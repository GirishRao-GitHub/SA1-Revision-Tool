1. Investment objective & guiding principle
  • Maximise return subject to acceptable risk
  • Match assets to liability nature (currency, term, benefit type)
  • Liability nature determines asset mix: level benefits → fixed income; salary-linked benefits → index-linked or real assets

  • Pre-funded liabilities tolerate more risk than immediate-needs liabilities
  • Benefits increase at 3% p.a. — inflation-linked assets preferred

  • Short-term bonds and money market instruments suitable for health
  • IRDAI admissibility rules constrain asset selection

2. Asset-liability modelling (ALM) process
  • Project liabilities via model office; select model points (enough for realism, few enough for run-time)
  • Liabilities typically projected deterministically; stochastic inflation possible for expenses
  • Decision on whether to include new business (unrealistic without it; uncertain to predict)
  • Choose trial investment distribution; project returns using stochastic model; vary mix and iterate
  • Optimisation criterion: maximise return subject to low probability of insolvency or capital breach
  • Dynamic interactions: lower returns reduce salary-linked benefits and expense inflation
  • Number of simulations (1,000–10,000) and time horizon (5–25 years) to be decided
  • All results checked for reasonableness
Mar21-Q1v
  • ALM results inform asset mix — particularly relevant for long-dated LTC liabilities
  • Results of ALM exercise needed before finalising asset allocation
Nov19-Q1iv
  • Existing ALM model (from non-health lines) can be adapted for health
  • Lag/delay table for claims development needed for liability projection
  • Durational matching tested under scenarios (±20% loss ratio, ±2% interest rate, ±20% volume)
  • Simulations identify mismatches beyond target range (e.g. ±5%); extreme scenarios covered by reserves
  • Currency impact considered if overseas (travel) cover included
Apr16-Q3ii
  • Non-unit reserve separate from unit fund; ALM applies to non-unit liability only
  • Unit fund moves with market — RSM also moves; ASM must mirror this
Jul22-Q1vi, Q1vii
  • Under Solvency II, BEL projection performed on best estimate assumptions
  • Stochastic or market-consistent simulation used where financial guarantees or options are embedded
Nov23-Q1vii
  • PVIF model projects future shareholder cashflows including future supervisory reserves and SCR
  • Unit and non-unit funds both projected for unit-linked products
  • Model points used if individual policy modelling impractical
Dec22-Q1v
3. Suitable asset classes by product type
  • Level benefits: short-dated fixed-interest bonds and cash
  • Salary-linked / increasing benefits: index-linked gilts; or real assets (equities, property) as alternative
  • Proprietary insurer: equities and property appropriate for free surplus to meet shareholder duty
  • Small/inexperienced insurer: avoid large proportion of volatile assets
  • Well-capitalised insurer: can take more risk and mismatch
May23-Q1ii; May25-Q3d
  • 3% p.a. increasing benefits: index-linked gilts preferred
  • Pre-funded portfolio: more scope for risky assets (long horizon)
  • Immediate-needs portfolio: more conservative; closer to payment
  • Short-dated gilts/cash for SCR backing to reduce market risk
  • Equity and property for free surplus; duty to shareholders
Nov19-Q1iv
  • Short-term bonds and money market instruments (short-tail liability)
  • IRDAI admissibility rules constrain eligible assets
  • Lower investment float than long-tail lines
Apr16-Q3ii
  • Unit fund: policyholder's chosen fund (equity, balanced, debt, etc.)
  • Non-unit fund: conventional short-term fixed income to back risk charges
  • Shareholder fund: mirror policyholder unit fund exposure proportionally for ULIP equity
Jul22-Q1vi, Q1vii
  • Fixed-interest: income and redemption known — model default allowance only
  • Index-linked: as fixed-interest but model future index changes
  • Equities: project current dividend stream; assume on market growth outlook
  • Property: project rental income and capital growth; allow for void, refurbishment, illiquidity
  • Money market: model as infinite income at short-term rates, or lump sum if imminent
  • Overseas assets: same approach with exchange rate and default risk adjustments
Sep21-Q2i
  • Investment return assumption needed for PVIF — must be consistent with liability assumptions
  • Market-consistent EV: risk-free investment return; separate allowance for non-hedgeable risk
Dec22-Q1v
4. Currency & overseas matching
  • Assets must be in same currency as liabilities
  • If policies cover overseas employees: match with overseas investments in that currency
May23-Q1ii; May25-Q3d
  • Same principle applies; need to know if overseas policyholders are covered
Nov19-Q1iv
  • Exchange rate impact of currency must be factored if travel/overseas cover is included
Apr16-Q3ii
  • Not the primary issue for unit-linked; unit fund currency follows fund currency
  • Correct risk-free reference rate depends on currency and timing of each cashflow
  • Overseas assets: allow for exchange rates and jurisdiction-specific default risk
Sep21-Q2i; Nov23-Q1vii
  • Currency of investment return assumption must match liability currency
5. Liquidity considerations
  • Growing company can use incoming premiums to pay claims — but future premiums not guaranteed
  • Volatile outgo → retain higher proportion of liquid assets
  • Large proportion of guaranteed business makes net outgo more volatile → more caution needed
May23-Q1ii; May25-Q3d
  • Same liquidity caution applies; illiquid assets (property) less suitable for immediate-needs portfolio
  • Pre-funded portfolio can tolerate illiquidity better given long horizon
Nov19-Q1iv
  • Short-tail health: claims paid quickly — high liquidity requirement
  • Lower float than long-tail lines limits ability to hold illiquid assets
Apr16-Q3ii
  • Unit fund is liquid (unit prices published daily/weekly)
  • Shareholder fund liquidity: cash preferred when solvency is marginally above minimum
Jul22-Q1vi
  • Volatility adjustment designed to prevent forced asset sales during extreme spread events
  • Liquidity plan required as condition for using volatility adjustment
Nov23-Q1vii
  • Not a primary EV consideration, but liquidity risk affects frictional costs in PVIF
6. Capital backing & solvency interaction
  • Short-dated gilts and cash appropriate to back SCR — reduces market risk exposure
  • Less critical for well-capitalised company with large free surplus relative to SCR
May23-Q1ii; May25-Q3d
  • Short-dated gilts/cash for SCR backing; proportion of risky assets depends on size of free surplus vs SCR
  • Very long-tail liability: significant capital strain; ALM challenge
Nov19-Q1iv
  • Capital model must account for investment income on health float (lower float than other lines)
  • Impact of health LOB on overall solvency ratio to be assessed
Apr16-Q3ii
  • RSM moves with unit fund market values; ASM must include equity in same proportion
  • If equity rises in unit fund and ASM has no equity, RSM increases without ASM support — solvency falls
  • When solvency marginally above minimum: cash preferred for excess assets
  • When solvency well above minimum: excess assets may include equity, bonds, property for higher return
Jul22-Q1vi, Q1vii
  • SCR released over time is part of PVIF in Solvency II EV
  • Risk margin = cost of holding SCR against non-hedgeable risks at 6% p.a. cost of capital rate
  • Matching adjustment reduces BEL by raising discount rate — reduces capital required
Sep21-Q2i; Nov23-Q1vii; Dec22-Q1iv
  • Net assets = assets in excess of technical provisions + SCR
  • Release of SCR and risk margin over time forms part of PVIF
  • Frictional costs (tax, agency costs) on cost of holding required capital deducted from PVIF
Dec22-Q1v
7. Matching adjustment & Solvency II discount rate
  • ALM exercise results inform whether matching adjustment may be applicable
  • LTC liabilities may qualify for matching adjustment: long-term, predictable, key risks are longevity and expense
  • Conditions: regulator approval; no surrender; ring-fenced assets; assets matched by nature, term and currency
  • Extent of matching adjustment = additional yield on backing assets less fundamental spread (default allowance)
  • Example: 50% of assets with +1.50% yield, deduct 0.50% default → matching adjustment = 0.50% p.a.
Dec22-Q1iv; Nov23-Q1vii
  • Short-tail health: typically ineligible for matching adjustment (not long-term predictable)
  • Risk-free discount rate applies; volatility adjustment may be considered
  • Unit fund reserve: unit values at market — no matching adjustment relevant
  • Non-unit reserve: risk-free discount rate applies
  • Risk-free rate term structure used for all BEL discounting
  • Matching adjustment: additional yield on backing bonds less fundamental spread
  • Volatility adjustment: added to risk-free rate where MA not eligible; subject to supervisory approval and risk management conditions
  • Both adjustments must be approved by regulator
Nov23-Q1vii; Dec22-Q1iv
  • Investment return assumption consistent with discount rate basis chosen (market-consistent or traditional)
  • If MA applied to BEL, the higher discount rate reduces BEL and increases net assets
Dec22-Q1iv-v
8. Investment income estimation (Solvency II)
  • Investment return assumptions must be consistent with liability cashflow assumptions
  • Long duration: need to project income over extended horizon
  • Index-linked: model future index changes for benefits increasing at 3% p.a.
  • Short horizon: investment income projections simpler but float is limited
  • Unit fund income: fund manager projects returns by asset class within fund
  • Non-unit fund income: conventional fixed-income projection
  • Allow for investment expenses on all assets
  • Fixed-interest: known income and redemption; allow for default on non-government issues
  • Index-linked: as fixed-interest plus estimate of future index changes
  • Equities: project dividend stream using economic outlook; model assumptions for anticipated sales
  • Property: project rental income and capital growth; allow for void, refurbishment, illiquidity
  • Money market: model as income stream at short rates or as lump sum if imminent claims
  • Overseas: same approach with exchange rate and default risk adjustments
  • Assumptions consistent with those used for liability cashflows
Sep21-Q2i
  • Investment return assumption feeds PVIF projection; must match discount rate basis
  • Market-consistent: risk-free rates only; non-hedgeable risks treated separately
Dec22-Q1v
9. Unit-linked specific investment considerations
  • Not applicable to conventional IP
  • Unit-linked LTCI: unit and non-unit fund both projected; non-unit cashflows depend on unit fund size
  • Market-consistent EV applies to unit-linked LTCI block
Dec22-Q1v
  • Not applicable to conventional PMI
  • Investment risk borne by policyholder; insurer bears only non-unit risk
  • Risk premium reinsurance appropriate (not investment-linked reinsurance)
  • Capital requirements low if risk borne by customer; charging structure determines extent
  • Poor recent equity performance may depress demand — consider promoting fixed-interest fund options
  • Premium charges deducted from unit fund: risk charges, expenses, taxes all charged against units
  • ASM must include equity in proportion to ULIP equity fund to maintain RSM/ASM balance
Jul22-Q1vi, Q1vii; May24-Q2ic
  • For unit-linked, financial guarantees may require stochastic / market-consistent simulation for BEL
  • Risk margin uses cost of capital method on non-hedgeable risks only (market risk hedged via unit fund)
Nov23-Q1vii
  • Unit-linked EV: unit fund projected separately from non-unit; PVIF depends on both
  • Market-consistent basis standard for unit-linked EV
Dec22-Q1v
10. Further information needed to refine asset mix
  • Mix of business and how benefits increase (level vs salary-linked)
  • Whether policies cover overseas employees (currency)
  • Size and investment expertise of insurer
  • Size of free surplus relative to SCR
  • Recent claims experience and variability
  • Extent of guaranteed vs reviewable benefits
  • Tax position of the company
  • Legislative investment restrictions (IRDAI)
  • Results of any existing ALM exercise
May23-Q1ii; May25-Q3d

  • Proportion of pre-funded vs immediate needs in portfolio
  • Same further information points as IP apply

  • Whether overseas / travel cover is included (currency exposure)
  • IRDAI admissibility rules for specific asset classes
Apr16-Q3ii
  • Size of ULIP equity fund relative to total policyholder fund
  • Solvency ratio: level above/below regulatory minimum
  • Fund performance history and policyholder behaviour assumptions
Jul22-Q1vi, Q1vii

  • Regulator-published fundamental spreads for matching adjustment calculation

  • Contract boundary definition (affects which future premiums are included in BEL)
