M
Monitor actual vs expected
½ mk
▼
  • Compare actual [experience rate] against that assumed in the premium basis and reserving basis
  • Monitor separately by product, channel, duration, age band — do not aggregate away heterogeneity
  • Name the specific metric in your answer: actual A/E ratio, claim inception rates, lapse rates by duration, expense per policy etc.
A
Analyse surplus — isolate the experience loss
1–1½ mk
▼
  • Perform an analysis of surplus to quantify the loss/gain attributable to [experience item] alone, separately from other sources (investment, lapse, expense, mortality etc.)
  • This puts the experience in context — is the magnitude sufficient to justify remedial action?
  • Distinguish between model error (wrong structure), parameter error (right structure, wrong numbers), and random fluctuations
C
Consistency — examine trends over time
½ mk
▼
  • Look at past data: is the experience deviation persistent and worsening, or a one-off fluctuation?
  • Persistent = systemic problem → structural action needed; one-off = monitor and hold
  • Sufficient data credibility? If portfolio is small, consider industry/reinsurer data as supplement
F→
Feed back revised assumptions into FPR
1½–2 mk
▼
P — Pricing basis
Revise premium rates / UL charges upward. For reviewable contracts, apply to in-force book immediately. Prevents losses on future new business.
R — Reserving basis
Strengthen reserves. Prevents over-distribution of surplus to capital providers. Protects solvency of in-force book.
R — Risk management
Update ruin/capital models. Revise reinsurance retention and treaty terms. Reassess risk appetite and stress tests.
Key exam phrase — always use this
  • "Revised assumptions fed back into decision-making procedures, including the pricing basis (leading to revised premiums/charges), the reserving basis (preventing over-distribution of profit), and risk management models (e.g. reinsurance policy)."
!
Part (a) exam tips
watch
▼
  • Max is usually 3–5 marks — don't over-write. Two well-explained feedback arm points suffice.
  • Must mention analysis of surplus by name — "compare actual vs expected" alone is not sufficient for the full mark.
  • Always flag reviewable contracts in the pricing feedback arm — examiners reward it consistently.
  • Part (a) = the ACC diagnostic loop only. Management actions belong in Part (b).

P
Product redesign
2–3 mk
▼
Generic sub-actions (list 3–4 relevant ones)
  • Policy wording / benefit definition — tighten or broaden definition of insured event
  • Reduce guarantees and options — limits exposure to future adverse trends
  • Introduce or strengthen premium/charge review facility — allows repricing of in-force book
  • Switch from with-profits to unit-linked — transfers risk, easier to adjust charges
  • Add partial/proportionate benefits (e.g. return-to-work for IP, sub-limits for expenses)
  • Shorten linked-claims period / contractual benefit payment period
  • Introduce market value adjustment or smoothing for investment-linked products
  • Add no-claims discount / bonus to incentivise good behaviour
R
Reinsurance strategy revision
½–1 mk
▼
  • Surplus treaty — on original terms (OT) or risk premium (RP) basis for morbidity/mortality
  • Catastrophe reinsurance — for mortality shocks or pandemic scenarios
  • Quota share — releases capital, reduces concentration risk
  • Stop-loss / aggregate XL — caps total claims in a year
  • Financial reinsurance — for investment or expense experience issues
  • Recalculate optimal retention using revised experience assumptions
U
Underwriting strategy
½–1 mk
▼
  • Review whether underwriting strategy is consistent with pricing assumptions — misalignment is a common driver of losses
  • Tighten medical evidence requirements, increase NML limits, add exclusions or ratings
  • For NB volumes shortfall: consider relaxing underwriting judiciously (improved offer rates) — but monitor adverse selection risk
  • Review underwriting by channel — broker vs bancassurance vs direct may need different approaches
D
Distribution & selling
½–1 mk
▼
  • Focus selling on lives with genuine financial need — reduces selective withdrawal (healthy lives lapse less)
  • Review channel mix — high-lapse or adverse-selection channels may need restructuring
  • Review intermediary incentives — misaligned commission structures drive churn and adverse selection
  • For NB volumes: add new channels, strengthen intermediary relationships, improve persistency commissions
  • For NB mix: redirect distribution toward target segments; use tiered commission to incentivise right mix
E
Expenses & operations
½–2 mk (primary for expense experience)
▼
  • Cost reduction programs — headcount review, procurement, non-essential spend
  • Outsourcing — non-core functions (administration, IT, claims processing)
  • Process automation / technology — STP rates, digital servicing, reduce unit costs
  • Economies of scale — increase new business volumes to spread fixed costs
  • Monitor and report unit costs per policy vs budget monthly — tighten management information
  • Benchmark against industry norms to identify specific cost lines out of line
S
Specific controls (experience-dependent)
1–2 mk
▼
Morbidity / Mortality
  • Claims reassessment — more frequent, independent recertification of claimant status
  • Rehabilitation — counselling / back-to-work programmes if cost-effective (savings > cost)
  • Death claim contestability review — verify non-disclosure, check policy exclusions
Lapse / persistency
  • Customer retention — proactive contact before lapse, surrender reconsideration process
  • Competitive analysis — are lapses driven by competitor products? pricing? service failure?
Investment returns
  • Improve asset-liability matching — reduce duration mismatch, reduce reinvestment risk
  • Use derivatives / hedging to limit downside on guaranteed return products
  • Revise investment mandate — reassess risk/return profile vs guarantees
New business mix
  • Granular profitability analysis by segment — identify which cells are profitable vs loss-making
  • Introduce or refine segment-specific pricing to reflect actual mix of risks
