The old way — IGAAP
Under old Indian GAAP, the insurer split the premium into "earned" and "unearned" portions based on time. Simple. Mechanical. But it missed a lot of economic reality.

What IGAAP missed: It did not separately show (1) how much risk the insurer was actually bearing, (2) the profit locked in on future months, or (3) whether the policy was profitable at all. Regulators and investors were flying blind.

The jargon — plain English
Every new term in Ind AS 117 has a simple idea behind it. Here they are, translated.
ICL — Insurance Contract Liability
Simply: "what the insurer owes." It replaces the old words "reserves" and "unearned premium." It has three parts: LRC, RA, and CSM (for profitable contracts).
LRC — Liability for Remaining Coverage
Simply: "the cover we still have to provide." This is the best estimate of what future claims will cost. It shrinks each month as cover is given. Think of it as the "cost" portion of the old unearned premium.
LIC — Liability for Incurred Claims
Simply: "claims we know about but haven't paid yet." This includes RBNS (reported but not settled) and IBNR (incurred but not yet reported). Under Ind AS 117, IBNR is discounted to present value.
RA — Risk Adjustment
Simply: "a cushion for uncertainty." Claims could be worse than expected — this buffer is held on the balance sheet and released to profit only as time passes safely. It makes the insurer's risk-bearing visible for the first time.
CSM — Contractual Service Margin
Simply: "profit held in a box until it is earned." When a policy is written at a profit, that profit goes into the CSM (a liability). It is released to the P&L each month as cover is provided. A loss-making policy has no CSM — it has a "loss component" instead, recognised immediately.
PAA — Premium Allocation Approach
Simply: "the simplified method for short policies." Annual health policies qualify for PAA because the cover period is 12 months or less. Under PAA, you don't need to separately calculate CSM — it works much like the old unearned premium approach, but you still show the RA and LIC separately.
GMM — General Measurement Model
Simply: "the full method for long-term policies." Used for critical illness riders, multi-year top-up covers. Requires explicit CSM calculation and discounting. More complex but gives a richer picture of long-term profitability.
Insurance revenue (not GWP)
Simply: "the portion of premium you have actually earned this period." Under old IGAAP, the top line was Gross Written Premium — all cash collected. Under Ind AS 117, the top line is only what has been earned by providing cover. A company with ₹4,500 Cr GWP might show only ₹4,200 Cr as revenue. Neither is wrong — they measure different things.
