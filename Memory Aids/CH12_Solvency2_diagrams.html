The Solvency II Three-Pillar Framework

The three pillars work together: quantitative standards, qualitative governance, and market transparency.

Figure 12.1

Solvency II Balance Sheet — Health & Care Insurer

Assets must at minimum cover technical provisions (BEL + Risk Margin), MCR and SCR. Surplus sits above SCR. Ineligible capital cannot be counted.

Figure 12.2

Valuation of Technical Provisions

Hedgeable cashflows are valued at market price directly. Non-hedgeable cashflows require a BEL (discounted expected cashflows) plus a Risk Margin (cost-of-capital method at 6% p.a.).

Figure 12.3

Standard Formula SCR — Insurance Risk Sub-Module Structure

The SCR is built up from individual risk modules, aggregated using prescribed correlation matrices. For SA1, focus is on the Health insurance module. Life and Non-Life are shown for completeness.

Figure 12.4

Supervisory Intervention Levels

Two rungs of a 'ladder'. Breaching the SCR triggers a recovery plan (6 months to rectify). Breaching the MCR triggers a short-term finance scheme (3 months) — ultimately, the company may lose authorisation.
