Insurance & Financial Options

Strictly from the SP1 / SA1 revision notes · Three revision modes

Insurance Options Policyholder rights — no re-underwriting
Financial Options Embedded financial guarantees
Key Distinction The rule to remember
Insurance options are about anti-selection risk — sicker people exercise them, so the insurer pays claims at standard rates for sub-standard lives. Cost ≈ take-up rate × health disadvantage of exercisers.

Financial options are about investment / economic risk — the insurer has promised a return or benefit level that may be expensive if markets move adversely. Valued using market-consistent / risk-neutral pricing, not cashflow projections.

LTCI is the main exception in H&C insurance where financial options matter — because reserves are large enough for investment guarantees to be material. For CI, IP and PMI the reserves are usually too small for financial options to be significant.
