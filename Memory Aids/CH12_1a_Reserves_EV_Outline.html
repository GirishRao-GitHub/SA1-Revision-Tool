Policy ReserveEPV future claims + expenses − EPV future premiums · Active life reserve / prospective premium reserve
Claims Reserve (IBNR)Incurred but not reported · beware late CI claims (policyholder learns of cover retrospectively)
Reported Not SettledClaims in assessment · IP in payment · CI in assessment period

Reserve Types — Short-Term Insurance (PMI)
UPRUnearned Premium Reserve · retrospective · proportion of risk-related premium for unexpired period
URRUnexpired Risk Reserve · prospective · held when UPR < expected future claims + expenses
IBNRIncurred But Not Reported · statistical methods (chain ladder) · small for PMI
Claims in TransitReported but not assessed / not recorded · pipeline claims
Outstanding ClaimsNotified but not fully settled · case or statistical estimate
IBNERIncurred But Not Enough Reported · adjustment (+ or −) to outstanding claims reserve
Equalisation / CatAtypical years · smooth profits · legislation varies by country · may get favourable tax treatment
Short-term: premium terms reset at each renewal → no need for policy reserve beyond next renewal

Reserving Methods — Case vs Statistical
CASE ESTIMATESClaims manager inspects each file individually · procedure type · hospital · surgeon · policy coverage · age/gender/history · medical inflation
Case: When to UseSmall volumes · large / unusual claims · low-data situations · can't do IBNR by case
Case: Key RiskDepends on assessor skill · bias (conservative/optimistic/negotiation) · expensive · hard to checkSTATISTICALHomogeneous cohorts · based on historical trends · chain ladder run-off triangles · segment by contract / distribution / geography
Statistical: When to UseLarge stable portfolio · majority of PMI claims · includes IBNR if data structured correctly
Long-TermKnown lump sum (CI) · income duration unknown (IP/LTCI) → statistical or deterministic/stochastic models
In practice: statistical for bulk + case estimates for large/unusual outliers

Embedded Value — Concept & Calculation
DefinitionPV of future shareholder profits on existing business + release of shareholder net assets
Two Components① Shareholder net assets (market value less lock-in discount for capital constraints)② PVFP — Present Value of Future (shareholder) Profits on in-force business
PVFP LogicEV basis more realistic than reserving basis → margins release as profit · if bases identical → PVFP = 0
Business TypesWithout-profits: PV(premiums + inv income − claims − expenses + reserve release)Unit-linked: PV(charges − expenses − excess benefits + non-unit reserve release)
Discount RateRisk discount rate reflects required return · locked-in capital discounted at RDR (worth less than market value)
EV most relevant to long-term business · concept introduced in earlier assumptions chapters

Appraisal Value & Key Relationships
Appraisal ValueEV + Goodwill · used in M&A of long-term insurers
GoodwillPV of profits from future NEW business · brand + expertise · beyond scope of SP1
EV vs Reserving BasisReserves: prudent · EV: realistic · margin between = source of PVFP
Purpose → BasisSolvency: prudent · Management: best estimate · M&A: best estimate ± buyer/seller bias
EV = in-force value only · Appraisal Value = EV + value of future new business (goodwill)
