Regulatory Objectives
Why Regulate? Advance payment → trust · Insurer may collapse · Unscrupulous operators · Difficulty monitoring solvency
Regulator Goals Safety & soundness · Consumer protection · Appropriate outcomes · Fair competition · Financial system integrity · Industry development
Balancing Act Too little → risk & loss · Too much → ↑ premiums, ↓ competition, ↓ coverage
Drivers of Change Economic environment (2008 crisis) · Harmonisation desire (Solvency II) · Risk management advances

Business Standards Senior mgmt systems & controls · Market conduct · Treating Customers Fairly (TCF)

Authorisation & Business Standards Detail
Authorisation By class of business · Separate application per class · EU: authorised in one state → passport across EU
Product Pre-Approval Some jurisdictions require upfront sign-off · Others expect discussion of high-risk / novel products
Senior Mgmt Controls Compliance · Risk mgmt · Systems & controls · Outsourcing · Fit & proper appointment
TCF — Market Conduct Communications: clear, fair, not misleading · Info & remuneration disclosure · Product info at point of sale · Compensation rules
Other Rules Suitability assessment · Record-keeping · Right to cancel · Claims handling · Unit-linked assets

Valuation, Capital Requirements & Regulator Actions
Asset Valuation Market vs book · Unlisted assets · Admissibility limits
Liabilities Types of business · Discretionary increases · Options & guarantees
Assumptions Discount rates · Demographic derivation · Persistency · Options/guarantees valuation
Capital Requirements Minimum levels · Supervisory intervention triggers · Long-term fund vs shareholder fund
Regulator Powers Remedy situation · Public censure · Fines · Req. reinsurance/capital · Revoke permission · Merger · Criminal prosecution
Proportionality Actions ∝ nature, scale & complexity of risk · Discretion vs rule-based triggers vary by jurisdiction

Equality Legislation
Protected Characteristics Age · Disability · Gender · Gender reassignment · Marriage & civil partnership · Pregnancy & maternity · Race · Religion & belief · Sexual orientation
Direct vs Indirect Direct: less favourable treatment · Indirect: neutral policy with disproportionate impact
Age Age bands OK if based on risk data · Age limits permitted via signposting · Maternity cost differences unlawful
Disability Special terms OK if based on relevant, reliable data · Physical/mental impairment with long-term substantial effect
Gender (EU) EU Gender Directive: no gender rating for new business from Dec 2012 · Proxy factors (correlated to gender) also not permitted · Contingency loadings needed for uncertain gender mix
Proportionate means to a legitimate aim = justification test for differential treatment
