② Long-Term · ③ Short-Term — Types of Reserve
Reserve Types by Contract Duration
Long-Term InsuranceCI · IP · LTCI · Group LT
  • Policy Reserve (Active Life Reserve)
    EPV future claims + expenses − EPV future premiums.
    Held for instances when future outgo > future income.
  • IBNR — Incurred But Not Reported
    Claims arising but not yet notified. Special risk for CI: policyholders may learn of cover years after the disability event (late notification increasingly common as conditions have expanded and brokers more involved).
  • Reported But Not Settled (RBNS)
    Claims notified, in assessment or in payment (e.g. IP claims in deferred period, CI in assessment).
  • Option Reserve
    Set aside for the event an option "comes into the money." Example: guaranteed conversion options on CI policies.
  • Group Context: UPR + URR also apply (as defined under short-term).
  • Investment Mismatching Reserve: Required in some territories where assets don't match liabilities.
Active life reserves = reserves for policyholders not yet claiming. Disabled life reserves = reserves for those currently claiming.
Short-Term InsurancePMI · Annual Group
  • UPR — Unearned Premium Reserve
    Retrospective. Balance of premiums received for unexpired risk. = (100−m)% × risk-related premium, where m% of risk falls before year-end. Risk-related premium excludes initial expenses (commission etc.) which are earned at inception.
  • URR — Unexpired Risk Reserve
    Prospective. Held when UPR < expected future claims + expenses for unexpired period. Estimated via projected future loss ratio applied to unexpired premium proportion.
  • IBNR
    As per long-term. Usually small for PMI. Calculated via statistical methods (e.g. chain ladder).
  • Claims in Transit
    Reported but not yet assessed or recorded ("pipeline claims"). Statistical or crude % adjustment.
  • Outstanding Claims Reserve
    Notified, not yet fully settled. Statistical methods or individual case estimates.
  • IBNER — Incurred But Not Enough Reported
    Adjustment (+ or −) to outstanding claims reserve where past case estimates have shown systematic bias. Separate identification aids management insight, may receive different tax treatment.
  • Equalisation / Catastrophe Reserve: Atypical years. Equalisation smooths profits across years. Cat reserve for future catastrophe events. Legislation and tax treatment vary by territory.
Short-term: premium and terms reset at each renewal → no need for policy reserve beyond next renewal date.
④ Reserving Methods — Case Estimates vs Statistical Estimation
The Two Primary Methods
Case Estimates
Experienced claims manager inspects each claim file and estimates ultimate outgo individually.
Factors considered (PMI / indemnity)
  • Procedure type → cost + likely in-patient duration
  • Hospital / medical centre to be used
  • Surgeon / consultant / medical principal
  • Policy coverage (full indemnity, excess, limits, recuperation benefit)
  • Age, gender, past claims history of claimant
  • Current levels of medical inflation
Advantages
Uses all known data · qualitative factors · applicable when stats unreliable
Disadvantages
Skill-dependent · potential bias (conservative/optimistic/negotiation) · expensive · hard to check · cannot do IBNR by case · inconsistent inflation rates
Statistical Estimation
Aggregate outstanding claims assessed in homogeneous cohorts using historical trends, adjusting for known future changes.
Conditions for use
  • Homogeneous claims portfolio
  • Portfolio large enough for credible data
  • Experience deemed stable
Segment by contract type · distribution type · geographical region. Fit statistical distribution to past experience. Apply earned premium as exposure base.
Run-off triangles (chain ladder) — main technique from CS2. If IBNR claims included separately in historic data compilation, model automatically captures IBNR.
LT Insurance: Benefit amount known for CI once submitted. IP/LTCI income duration unknown → statistical or deterministic/stochastic models for future claims outgo.
PMI Hybrid: Statistical for bulk + case estimates for large/unusual individual claims (which distort average cost). More important to use case estimates for smaller PMI insurers.
⑤ Embedded Value — Calculation in Detail
Embedded Value = Net Assets + PVFP
PV of future shareholder profits · existing business only
Component 1 — Net Assets
  • Excess of assets held over those required to meet liabilities (shareholder-owned share).
  • May be valued at market value, but locked-in capital is worth less than market value to shareholders.
  • Lock-in discount: Required capital must be invested cautiously (to meet SCR) → expected return < unconstrained return → discount by risk discount rate.
Lock-in example Capital = 100 · invested at 4% · RDR = 5% · 10yr lock-in
Discounted value = 100 × (1.04/1.05)¹⁰ = 90.874
Component 2 — PVFP (Present Value of Future Profits)
  • Project expected future cashflows on existing in-force business.
  • Discount at risk discount rate (RDR) to get PV.
  • Source of profit = gap between realistic EV assumptions and prudent reserving basis.
  • Key insight: If EV basis = reserving basis exactly → PVFP = 0.
Business TypePVFP Cashflows
Without-profitsPV(premiums + investment income − claims − expenses + reserve release)
Unit-linkedPV(charges inc. surrender penalties − expenses − benefits in excess of unit fund + non-unit reserve release)
Tax is allowed for within the calculation as appropriate. Reserves used for net assets must be consistent with those used in PVFP projection.
⑥ Purposes of EV · ⑦ Appraisal Value & Key Relationships
⑥ Purposes of Embedded Value
  • Internal management accounts: Realistic assessment of business value and management information.
  • Published financial statements: Included in external reporting.
  • M&A — appraisal value: EV is the major component; goodwill is added to arrive at appraisal value.
  • EV financing (financial reinsurance): Reinsurer advances a loan (expressed as commission) to insurer, repaid from future surplus stream from in-force business. Reflects prudence in valuation basis.
  • Staff / salesperson bonuses: Growth in EV used to assess and pay bonuses.
⑦ Appraisal Value & Key Relationships
Core relationship Appraisal Value = EV + Goodwill
EV = Net Assets + PVFP (in-force only)
Goodwill = PV of profits from future new business
  • EV scope: Existing (in-force) business only. Does not include value of future new business.
  • Goodwill: Brand + expertise → capacity to write profitable future new business. Valuation is subjective; beyond scope of SP1.
  • Basis gap is the engine: Prudent reserves → realistic EV → margin = PVFP. Wider gap → higher EV (but regulators watch).
