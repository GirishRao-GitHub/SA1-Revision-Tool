Chapter 13 — Assumptions (1)
General Considerations — Principles, Purpose & Basis Types
8 Principles Purpose of use · Financial significance · Consistency · Regulatory constraints · Client needs · Parameter accuracy from data · Data relevance to insured lives · Flexibility to reflect changing risk
4-Step Process ① Investigate historical experience → ② Assess future conditions → ③ Derive best estimates → ④ Apply margins for risk (size depends on purpose & risk degree)
Pricing Basis Best estimate + margins · 3 methods to allow for risk: margins in parameters / stochastic / higher RDR · 5 factors determining price: competitiveness · USP · risk appetite · data quality · free assets
Reserving Basis Best estimate + margins · Published accounts (going concern / true & fair) vs Solvency accounts (run-off / break-up) · Two approaches: prudent assumptions OR best estimate + explicit risk margin / SCR
EV / Profitability Basis Needs TWO bases: reserving basis (for net assets & reserve changes) + projection basis (for future profits) · Realistic unless purpose demands prudence (sale/purchase, EV financing, bonuses) · Lower RDR than pricing (volume uncertainty removed)
Consistency principle: pairs to watch — morbidity & lapses · morbidity & expenses · morbidity & investment return · NB volumes & fixed expenses · lapses & investment return · investment return & inflation

Chapter 14 — Assumptions (2)
Demographic Assumptions — Morbidity, Mortality & Lapses
Process (all demos) Collect data (adequate volume, limited heterogeneity, ~3–4 yrs) → Group into homogeneous risk cells → Calculate rates (standard table adjustments: i_x = a·i_x(S) + b) → Adjust for target market / UW / claim controls / trends
Claim Incidence IP: inception & recovery rates · CI: by illness type (cancer/HA/stroke/CABS/TPD) — project each separately before combining · LTCI: transitions able→claiming, lower→higher benefit · PMI: by benefit category · Social/economic factors: state welfare · economy/unemployment · inflation
Rating Factors Age · gender (may be prohibited) · smoker · occupation · benefit size · product class · individual vs group · distribution channel · region · duration since entry · UW status
Recovery Rates (IP) Crucial for IP; generally ignored for LTCI · Factors: keenness to return to work · economic environment · claim conditions / income ratio · insurer rehabilitation · cause of claim · large benefits → longer claims · medical advances (opposing effects on cost)
Mortality Separate rates: non-claiming healthy vs claiming sick · CI survivorship: mortality during survival period = adjustment to incidence rate (illness-specific) · For non-death-benefit products: do NOT over-estimate mortality (reduces exposure, under-prices risk)
Lapses Based on own data (market data less useful — heterogeneity / human choice) · Drivers: economic · political · commercial · awareness · Effect: early lapse = loss (NB strain unrecouped); late lapse = gain; selective lapsing = worsening morbidity in remaining pool · Lapse & re-entry risk when premiums fall
PMI renewal rates most sensitive to inflation (annual renewal, conscious decision) vs long-term products (guaranteed renewal, inertia). Market data poor for lapses — no population equivalent.

Chapter 15 — Assumptions (3)
Financial Assumptions — Benefits, Expenses, Commission & Investment
Benefit Amount LT (IP/CI/LTCI): pre-determined; priced per unit of benefit; large cases may need adjusted assumptions · PMI: must forecast average claim amount; own experience adjusted for policy conditions + hospital deals + future provider capacity trends
Benefit Inflation IP → earnings index · CI → fixed or mixed · LTCI → care costs index · PMI → medical inflation + treatment protocol changes + hospital charging + age profile of portfolio · Fixed-% avoids unknown future index; ceiling common for LTCI
Expenses (9 types) Initial acquisition · initial UW · initial admin · renewal admin · renewal commission · investment · withdrawal · claims admin · termination · PMI: investment & withdrawal not important · Loading: policy fee / sum insured differential / individual calc · Cross-subsidy risk between small & large policies
Expense Inflation Mix of price inflation + earnings inflation (majority = staff costs) · Consider: current rates · expected future rates · ILG vs nominal bond yield differential · recent company/industry experience · Apply to renewal, claims, termination loadings
Commission & Clawback Based on rates actually paid (market + legislation) · Higher for H&C than life (complexity + UW effort) · Clawback: credit risk on unearned commission — assume lapse rates & partial non-recovery · Loading: % of premium / % of SI / fixed sum
Investment Return Must be consistent with inflation · Significance ↑ with larger reserves + longer duration + heavier guarantees · PMI: unimportant · Single premium pre-funded LTCI: most critical · Asset mix: fixed interest (fixed benefit) / ILGs (real benefit) / equity (care cost inflation) · Market-consistent: risk-free rate
Taxes Profits tax: multiply future profit by (1 − tax rate) · I–E basis: investment income less allowable expenses, taxed net · Premium tax: neutral to insurer but reduces consumer demand → affects lapse & NB volume assumptions
Higher expenses vs life insurance: lower non-medical limits → more UW effort; product must be sold; claims management more intensive; claims in payment require ongoing monitoring & rehabilitation.

Chapter 16 — Assumptions (4)
Other Assumptions — New Business & Risk Discount Rate
NB Volume Needed for: (i) allocation of fixed expenses per policy · (ii) assessing capital adequacy to support NB strain · Based on past experience + market projections + competitive position
NB Mix Average policy size · By product · By distribution channel · By territory · By gender · By occupation · Cross-subsidy risk: if actual mix skews to higher-risk lives → anti-selection → monitor continuously · Required when uniform premiums charged to heterogeneous groups
Risk Discount Rate Two components: (1) Shareholders' required return = risk-free rate + market risk premium (via CAPM: E_i = r + β·[E_m − r]) · (2) Product-specific statistical risk: less/more risky than company average → lower/higher than E_i
6 Risk Factors ↑ RDR Lack of historical data · High guarantees · Policyholder options · Overhead costs · Complexity of design · Untested market · Statistical risk assessed by: analytical variance / sensitivity analysis / stochastic simulation / market comparison
Market Consistency Alternative approach: discount at risk-free rate (term-dependent, from swap curves / govt bonds) · Risk margins included in other parameters (morbidity, expenses) to reflect estimation uncertainty · Useful reasonableness check on traditional RDR approach
RDR must always exceed risk-free rate. CAPM gives company-level return; product-level adjustment for relative riskiness. Margins in parameters vs margins in RDR give equivalent result but express risk differently.
