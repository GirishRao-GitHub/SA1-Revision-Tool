M
Monitor actual vs expected morbidity
1 mk
▼
  • Compare actual claim inception/termination rates to those assumed in the premium basis and reserving basis
  • Monitor separately for each product type (CI, IP, LTC, PHI etc.) — don't aggregate
  • For unit-linked, also compare charges assumed vs actual morbidity cost
A
Analyse surplus — isolate morbidity P&L
1½ mk
▼
  • Perform an analysis of surplus to quantify the morbidity loss separately from other profit/loss sources (mortality, lapse, investment etc.)
  • This puts losses in context — is the magnitude sufficient to justify remedial action?
  • Distinguish between one-off fluctuations and systematic adverse experience
C
Consistency check — trends over time
½ mk
▼
  • Review past experience data — is the adverse morbidity persistent and worsening, or random?
  • This determines whether model/parameter error or random fluctuation is the likely cause
  • Persistent = systemic problem needing structural action; one-off = monitor and hold
F
Feed back revised assumptions into FPR
3 mk
▼
P — Pricing
Revise premium rates and/or unit-linked charges. For reviewable contracts, apply immediately. Prevents losses on future new business.
R — Reserving
Revise reserving basis upward. Prevents over-distribution of profit to capital providers. Protects solvency of in-force book.
R — Risk mgmt
Update risk/ruin models. Recalculate reinsurance needs (e.g. surplus treaty amounts) and revise reinsurance policy accordingly.
Mnemonic — Part (b)
PRUCCS
"Pretty Rough Underwriting Creates Claim Surprises"
Exam technique Always give the action AND its mechanism/rationale — every bullet is worth ½ mark, but examiner typically wants both the "what" and the "why/how".
P
Product redesign
3 mk
▼
  • Tighten definition of claim (policy wording) — reduces incidence of borderline/ambiguous claims
  • Introduce a partial return-to-work benefit — incentivises rehabilitation, reduces claim duration
  • Reduce guarantees and options — limits company exposure to future adverse trends
  • Introduce or extend premium/charge review facility — allows company to reprice in-force book
  • Shorten linked-claims period — reduces moral hazard for IP policies
  • Switch from with-profits to unit-linked design — transfers investment risk; easier to adjust charges
R
Reinsurance strategy revision
1 mk
▼
  • Consider a surplus reinsurance treaty to cap aggregate morbidity losses
  • Can be arranged on original terms (OT) basis or risk premium (RP) basis depending on cost/benefit
  • Recalculate optimal retention level using revised morbidity assumptions
U
Underwriting strategy revision
1 mk
▼
  • Review whether underwriting strategy is consistent with standard pricing assumptions
  • A mismatch between underwriting decisions and pricing morbidity rates is a common driver of losses
  • May need to tighten medical evidence requirements, exclusions, or ratings at point of sale
C
Claims control improvement
1 mk
▼
  • Reassess and recertify sickness status of claimants more frequently — reduces spurious long-term claims
  • Use independent medical examiners rather than claimant's own GP — more objective recertification
  • Introduce active case management for high-value/long-duration claims
C
Counselling / Rehabilitation services
½ mk
▼
  • Introduce rehabilitation and counselling programmes — only if cost-effective (cost of service < claims cost saved)
  • Particularly effective for mental health and musculoskeletal IP claims — fastest-growing claim categories in India
S
Selling / Distribution focus
½ mk
▼
  • Target sales to lives with genuine financial need rather than potential claimants
  • Reduces selective withdrawal: financially motivated lives lapse less, removing the healthy-lives-lapse bias
  • Better quality book improves morbidity experience over time
Generic Answer Framework Adapt language to the specific product type in the question. If IP/PHI: focus on recovery/rehabilitation, claim duration, return-to-work. If CI: focus on incidence rates, definition of claim. If LTC: focus on care needs assessment, linked claims period.
a
Part (a) — ACC template [3 marks]
MAC→FPR
▼
Opening — Monitor (½ mk)
  • "The extent of the problem can be identified by monitoring experience, comparing actual [morbidity/claim] rates against those assumed in the [premium/reserving] basis."
Core — Analysis of Surplus (1 mk)
  • "An analysis of surplus will be performed to quantify the loss attributable to adverse morbidity, separately from other profit/loss sources (investment, lapse, expense etc.)."
  • "This contextualises the loss and establishes whether it is sufficiently material to justify remedial action."
Consistency check (½ mk)
  • "Past trends should be examined to determine whether the problem is persistent (systematic parameter/model error) or a one-off random fluctuation."
Feedback arms — each ½ mk, max 3 bullets (1½ mk)
  • "Revised assumptions fed into pricing basis → premiums/charges revised upward; if contracts reviewable, applies to in-force book too."
  • "Revised assumptions fed into reserving basis → prevents over-distribution of surplus to capital providers; protects solvency."
  • "Revised assumptions fed into risk management models → reinsurance retention and treaty terms reconsidered."
b
Part (b) — Management template [10 marks]
PRUCCS
▼
P — Product redesign (up to 3 mk — pick any 3 sub-points)
  • "In the longer term, the product design could be changed: [tighten claim definition / partial return-to-work benefit / reduce guarantees / introduce charge review / shorten linked claims period / move to UL from WP]."
R — Reinsurance (1 mk)
  • "The reinsurance strategy could be revised — a surplus reinsurance treaty on an original terms or risk premium basis may be appropriate to cap aggregate morbidity exposure."
U — Underwriting (1 mk)
  • "The underwriting strategy could be reviewed to ensure consistency with pricing morbidity assumptions — an inconsistency is a significant driver of losses."
C — Claims control (1 mk)
  • "Claims control procedures could be improved — e.g. more frequent and independent recertification of sickness status to reduce spurious long-duration claims."
C — Counselling/Rehab (½ mk)
  • "Rehabilitation and counselling services could be introduced if cost-effective (cost of service < claims cost saved)."
S — Selling focus (½ mk)
  • "More focused selling based on financial need reduces selective withdrawal by healthy lives, thereby improving portfolio morbidity experience."
!
Common mistakes & exam tips
watch out
▼
  • Don't confuse (a) and (b) — (a) is purely about the ACC loop; (b) is specific remedial actions. Many candidates mix them.
  • Part (a) does not need many actions — it's only 3 marks. Two feedback arm bullets are typically sufficient; don't over-write.
  • Part (b) needs the "why" not just the "what" — "improve claims control" alone gets ½ mk; adding "by more frequent independent recertification" earns the second ½ mk.
  • Product redesign is a multi-bullet goldmine — list 3–4 sub-types quickly; each earns ½ mk up to a reasonable sub-max.
  • Reinsurance — always specify surplus treaty and whether OT or RP basis; vague "get more reinsurance" is not credit-worthy.
  • Reviewable contracts — always flag this in the pricing feedback arm; examiners reward it consistently.
