Investment Principles
Why Invest?Meet contractual liabilities · Generate returns for SH & PH · Support commercial success & security
What is the Core Principle?Maximise overall return subject to: meeting contractual & TCF obligations · recognising uncertainties · overall risk SH, Reg & PH are prepared to tolerate
How is Risk Balanced?Match assets to nature, term & currency of liabilities · Free assets allow departure from matched position · Appetite for risk depends on capital strength & diversification
Investment importance varies by product — low for short-term protection (PMI); high for pre-funded LTCI where reserves are significant

Aim of Asset-Liability Management
What does ALM deliver?Investment strategy aligned to market risk attitude · Risk budget (how much to deviate from cashflow matching) · Strategic asset allocation + hedging + ongoing rebalancing
Conditions that shape ALMCapital strength & diversification → risk tolerance · SCR target 150–200% · Solvency II: VA means cashflow matching ≠ liability hedge · ORSA: projections integral to regulatory compliance
Trade-offsHedge best-estimate liabilities only vs seek full balance sheet stabilisation · Return maximisation vs SCR minimisation · Investment strategy vs NB strategy vs risk mgmt strategy — optimised jointly
Available capital = MV assets − market-consistent liabilities · P&L attribution is a regulatory requirement under Solvency II internal model approval

Effect of Liabilities on Asset Selection — The Four Types
What are the four types?① Guaranteed monetary (fixed CI/IP) → fixed-interest bonds② Index-linked (IP escalating / expenses) → IL gilts or real assets③ Indemnity (PMI) → cash & short bonds④ Investment-linked (unit-linked) → underlying fund assets
Key NuancesPerfect matching rarely achievable or desirable · Bond terms often shorter than liabilities · Reinvestment risk on surplus premiums · Medical inflation often > RPI · NAE: no bonds available; equities volatile short-term · Unit-linked: ALM not a major issue⚠ Match nature AND term AND currency — always all three
Net liability outgo = benefit payments + expenses − premiums · Immunisation is a second-best to exact monetary matching

Mean Term, Inflation & Liquidity
What are the three constraints?Mean term mismatch · Inflation linkage of liabilities · Liquidity of asset portfolio
ConditionsAssets too long → rising yield risk (capital loss) · Assets too short → falling yield risk (reinvestment) · Short-term liabilities: treat inflation as fixed · Long-term liabilities: inflation is major consideration · Volatile claims / contracting portfolio → higher liquidity needed
Trade-offsLonger assets = higher expected return but duration risk · Reinsurance reduces claims variability but delays recoveries (tension with matching) · Regulatory framework adds further constraints: admissibility, mismatching reserves, currency rules, custodianship
Future solvency threatened whether or not liabilities are discounted — depends on future investment returns · Relying on future NB premiums to bridge term mismatch is dangerous

The ALM Process & Modelling
Why Model?Test investment strategies · Estimate insolvency probability · Determine free assets needed · Enable advance planning for condition changes
What are the Steps?① Project liability outgo · ② Project asset proceeds · ③ Compare per period · ④ Re-run with different assumptions · ⑤ Assess appropriateness · ⑥ Investigate alternatives
How: Deterministic vs Stochastic?Deterministic: pre-set values, sensitivity testing · Stochastic: parameters from distributions; ESG generates joint economic scenarios · Real-world calibration for ALM projections · Risk-neutral for liability valuation at future points · Time zero stress tests for immediate risks
ESG outputs: interest rates, inflation, equity/property/credit returns · Liability projection system + separate asset model · Solvency requirements must be incorporated at each future period

Derivatives in ALM
Why Use Derivatives?Physical assets unavailable / impractical · Reduce regulatory capital (SCR) · Stabilise balance sheet (hedge risk margin) · Lower dealing costs · Efficient acquisition or disposal of exposure
What Types?Equity put options · Interest rate swaps · Currency options · Inflation derivatives · Index futures
How: Static vs Dynamic?Static: protect against specific severe scenario — set and hold · Dynamic: portfolio rebalanced frequently as liability risk sensitivities change · Residual risks always present: counterparty · liquidity · operational · basis risk
Basis risk: derivative based on index rather than actual portfolio held · Local regulatory restrictions on use of derivatives must be considered

Effect of Free Assets on Asset Selection
What are free assets?Excess of assets over liabilities · Some held securely (MCR) → short-dated domestic govt bonds · Excess available for higher-returning assets (equities, property)
ConditionsGreater free assets → greater permissible mismatch · Constrained by: acceptable risk level · regulatory admissibility · MCR/SCR requirements · Why hold above statutory minimum? Buffer against adverse movements · Regulatory expectation · Competitive signal to brokers/PH/SH · Inherent business risks exceed statutory floor
Trade-offsUsing free assets to support mismatch → those assets at risk of loss · SH may prefer to reserve free assets for NB strain / product development · Greater freedom = greater responsibility to PH (TCF) and SH (return)
Free assets measured relative to: supervisory reserves · premiums written · ultimate liabilities from recent claims · absolute liability size · regulatory capital requirements

Estimating Investment Income by Asset Class
What are the approaches?Fixed-interest: known stream; reduce for default risk progressively · IL securities: as above + index estimate · Equities: current dividend + inflation/growth · Property: rental + growth; conservative on capital · Cash: income stream OR lump sum now OR hybrid · Overseas: as domestic + FX assumptions
Key NuancesAllow for investment expenses in all classes · Reinvestment rates matter for long projections · Equity capital value assumptions needed if equities to be sold · Property: high management costs; allow for obsolescence · Cash: if held for imminent claims → lump sum approach more appropriate · Short-term rates vary significantly year to year
Equities as infinite dividend stream only valid if value < free assets and all liability outgo covered from other asset income + redemptions

Suitability of Assets for Matching
What features determine suitability?Term · Currency · Nature (real/fixed) · Expected return · Security · Volatility · Marketability · Expertise · Portfolio size · Free asset size · Tax · Admissibility
Conditions — Suitable when…Govt bonds → monetary guaranteed liabilities · IL gilts → index-linked liabilities (note: time-lag issue) · Cash/short bonds → PMI & short-tail · Equities/property → free assets or long-term real liabilities · Unit fund assets → investment-linked · Swaps → very long-term where bond market shallow
Trade-offsWithout-profits long-term: matching straightforward in principle; availability of assets limits practice · NAE matching: equities best long-term fit but short-term volatility problematic · IL gilts: time-lag prevents perfect match · Inflation caps/floors: derivatives possible but challenging
Unit-linked: straightforward — assets and liabilities matched directly · Alternative assets (infrastructure, equity release, private credit) increasingly used for very long-term business
