Investment Principles
Why Invest? Meet contractual liabilities · Generate returns for SH & PH · Support commercial success & security What is the Core Principle? Maximise overall return subject to: meeting all contractual & TCF obligations · recognising uncertainties · overall risk SH, Reg & PH are prepared to tolerate How is Risk Balanced? Closer matching → lower investment risk · Free assets allow departure from matched position · Appetite for risk depends on capital strength & diversification
Importance of investment varies by product — low for short-term protection (PMI); high for pre-funded LTCI

Aim of Asset-Liability Management
Why ALM? Derive investment strategy aligned to market risk attitude · Manage required capital · Stabilise full balance sheet What does ALM Deliver? Risk budget (how much to deviate from cashflow matching) · Solvency II: SCR reduction via matching · Hedge best-estimate liabilities or seek further BS stabilisation How is it Governed? Quantitative metrics (e.g. prob free capital < 0 over 5 yrs) · In conjunction with risk mgmt & NB strategy · Measuring, monitoring & rebalancing positions
Available capital = MV assets less market-consistent liabilities · Solvency cover ratio (SCR) target typically 150–200%

Effect of Liabilities on Asset Selection
Why Does Liability Type Matter? Nature determines matching asset · Term determines when proceeds needed · Currency determines asset currency What Are the Four Liability Types? Guaranteed monetary Index-linked (prices / earnings) Indemnity (PMI) Investment-linked (unit-linked) How to Match Each? Monetary → fixed-interest bonds Index → index-linked gilts / real assets Indemnity → cash & short bonds Unit-linked → underlying fund assets ⚠ Perfect matching rarely achievable or desirable
Also consider: free assets (allow mismatch) · regulatory constraints · mean term of assets vs liabilities · inflation · liquidity needs
↓
④
Mean Term, Inflation & Liquidity
Why Mean Term Matters Assets too long → exposed to rising yields (capital loss) · Assets too short → exposed to falling yields (reinvestment risk) What Are the Inflation Risks? Benefits linked to index · Indemnity (PMI) costs · Expense settlements · Short-term: treat as fixed · Long-term: inflation is major consideration How to Manage Liquidity Volatile claims → maintain liquid assets · Reinsurance reduces variability but delays recoveries · Contracting portfolio → can't fund claims from premiums
Regulatory framework: restrictions on asset types, solvency admissibility, currency matching, mismatching reserves & custodianship

The ALM Process & Modelling
Why Model? Test investment strategies · Estimate insolvency probability · Determine free asset levels needed · Enable advance planning What Are the Steps? ① Project liability outgo · ② Project asset proceeds · ③ Compare per period · ④ Re-run with different assumptions · ⑤ Assess appropriateness · ⑥ Investigate alternatives How: Deterministic vs Stochastic? Deterministic: pre-set values, sensitivity testing Stochastic: parameters randomly drawn from distributions · ESG generates economic scenarios Real-world calibration for ALM · Risk-neutral for valuation
ESG outputs: interest rates, inflation, equity / property / credit returns · Liability projection system + separate asset model · Time zero stress tests also used

Derivatives in ALM
Why Use Derivatives? Physical assets unavailable or impractical · Reduce regulatory capital · Stabilise balance sheet (hedge SCR / risk margin) · Lower tax & dealing costs · Efficient acquisition / disposal What Types / Strategies? Equity put options (limit downside) · Interest rate swaps (duration matching) · Currency options (FX hedging) · Inflation derivatives · Index futures (quick exposure) How: Static vs Dynamic Hedging? Static: protect against specific severe scenario · Dynamic: portfolio held & frequently rebalanced as conditions change
Residual risks: counterparty risk · liquidity risk · operational risk · basis risk (index vs actual portfolio) · local regulatory restrictions

Effect of Free Assets on Asset Selection
Why Free Assets Matter? Enable departure from matching · Cushion against insolvency · Benefit PH (lower premiums) & SH (higher dividends) What Can Free Assets Back? Minimum solvency capital (secure assets) · Excess: invest for long-term return · Equities (domestic & overseas) · Direct property · Benchmarked vs supervisory reserves, premiums written, liabilities, regulatory capital How Much Freedom? Greater free assets → greater permissible mismatch · Constrained by acceptable risk level · Regulatory framework limits strategy
Capital requirements effect: hold short-dated govt bonds for MCR backing (security + low volatility) · Insurers target well above statutory minimum

⑧
Estimating Investment Income by Asset Class
Why Estimate Income? Feed into liability projection model · Compare against liability outgo · Inform ALM strategy selection What Are the Key Approaches? Fixed-interest: known stream (allow for default) · Index-linked: adjust by index estimate · Equities: current dividend + growth assumption · Property: rental + growth; allow for illiquidity & expenses · Cash: rolling short-term rates · Overseas: + FX assumptions How: Volatility by Asset Class? Cash: nil capital volatility · Short bonds: very low · Long bonds: high (duration risk) · Index-linked: high capital, low income · Equities: very high capital · Property: moderate income, low day-to-day capital · Overseas: + currency volatility
Reinvestment rates important · Cash modelled as income stream OR lump sum now · Allow for investment expenses across all asset classes
↓
⑨
Suitability of Assets for Matching
Why Assess Suitability? Match liability cashflows · Optimise risk/return within constraints · Regulatory admissibility What Features Determine Suitability? Term · Currency · Nature (real/fixed) · Expected return · Security · Volatility · Marketability · Expertise required · Portfolio size · Free asset size · Tax · Admissibility How: Asset–Liability Pairs? Govt bonds → monetary guaranteed · IL gilts → index-linked · Equities / property → long-term real · Cash / short bonds → PMI & short-tail · Unit fund assets → investment-linked · Swaps → very long-term (LTCI / deferred annuities)
Matching difficulty: without-profits long-term (asset availability) · Unit-linked (straightforward) · Index-linked (time-lag problem) · NAE (no bond market; equities too volatile short-term)
