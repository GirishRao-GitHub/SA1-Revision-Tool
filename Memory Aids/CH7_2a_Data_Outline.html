Data Criteria & General Considerations
4 Core Criteria Relevant · Credible · Available · Reliable
Also Consider Cost of obtaining & analysing · Suitability of format
Relevance means Similar class of lives & policy conditions to the product in question
Credibility means Sufficient volume → low sampling error · Actuary combines sources using judgement
Adjustments Data often needs adjustment · Broad-based ratios initially · Improves as own experience grows
Primary focus: pricing · also relevant for reserving & profitability assessment

Projection to Mid-Point of Insurance Usage
Why Project? Data is historic · Must be valid across the full period rates are in force
Project Forward By ½ × [shelf-life of rates] + ½ × [expected policy duration to termination / next review]
Claim Inception Rates Vary by age & calendar year · Project using annual improvement factors
Termination Rates Vary by duration from claim start · Allow for improvement or deterioration
PMI Avg Claim Size Function of inflation, protocols, hospital charges, State provision · Reviewed annually → shorter horizon
Shorter Term Benefit Shorter review cycle → smaller uncertainty margins → cheaper premiums & smaller reserves
If future uncertainty is high, contract should allow for periodic review — otherwise reserves make premiums uncompetitive

Own Data
Best For Relevance — reflects company's own underwriting, claims management, distribution & target market
Relevance Factors Underwriting & acceptance · Policy conditions · Claims management · Distribution channels · Target market
Cost / Format No problem — held internally
Credibility Gap Volume usually insufficient, especially at risk-cell level → must supplement with external data
Workaround Fit simple adjustment to standard table · Use market data to weight low-credibility cells
New Insurer Totally reliant on external sources for years until own data builds credibility
Own data: high relevance, low credibility — opposite problem to population data
↓
④
Population Data
Source National government periodic healthcare analyses · Free & readily available · Large volumes → credible
Drawbacks Accuracy/reliability questionable (double counting) · Format may be unsuitable · Often out-of-date on publication
Relevance — Lives Not specific to insured lives · Improve by splitting by socio-economic group & region
Relevance — Definition Population 'sickness' definition ≠ insurance claim definition · Must adjust
Anti-selection Nuance Underwriting → healthier insured lives; but non-essential policy → purchasers may expect to claim → offset · No universal rule
Vital first estimate for some risk cells · may be only available base · still needs adjustment before use
↓
⑤
Reinsurers' Data
Why Provided Part of reinsurer's service · Reinsurer profits depend on accurate pricing → incentive to help
Payment Normally via reinsurance treaty (share of business) rather than direct fee
Relevance Must adjust for company's own underwriting & claims management practices
Credibility Draws on broad market experience · Low for brand-new products · Grows as market for product develops
Classic Use Case CI insurance launch in new territory — reinsurers bridged gap using weighted overseas + local population data
Reinsurers are market-wide 'data experts' — especially valuable at product launch
↓
⑥
Market Data & Other Sources
Insured Lives Data Industry pooling (eg CMI in UK) · More credible than own · Less relevant than own · Produces standard morbidity tables · Takes time to publish
Supervisory Returns Accurate overall · Limited detail for pricing · High-level cross-check only · Not designed for competitors
Overseas Data Less relevant (culture, State care, legislation, policy conditions differ) · May be credible in volume · Use with caution · Bridge via population ratios
Actuarial Consultants Like reinsurers (national & international data) · Charge a fee · Do not share in the risk
Rate Table Software Market premium comparisons · Useful for initial data & competitiveness checks
Trade Magazines Market info & benefit/premium levels · Reasonability check · No claim statistics
Insured lives data: credibility between own data & population data · relevance between own data & population data
