⚠️ Common Pitfalls

  • Generic lists: "Consider claims experience" scores 0. Say what aspect of claims experience and why it matters for this specific product.
  • Missing anti-selection: Almost every product design question has marks for anti-selection risk. Don't skip it.
  • Forgetting TCF / regulation: At least 2–3 marks in any 25+ question are on regulatory / consumer protection considerations.
  • Ignoring existing policyholders (redesign Qs): If the question is about redesign, marks are always available for treating existing policyholders fairly and closed vs open book management.
  • Not using the scenario: Read the scenario carefully — product type, market, customer demographics. Every point should be anchored in the context.
  • Over-weighting pricing: Pricing is just one of 9 themes. Don't write 80% of your answer on pricing and skip distribution, operations, and lapse risk.

✓ Examiner's Expectations

  • Structure matters: Use clear headings for each theme. Examiners mark from a pre-prepared schema — help them find your marks.
  • Both sides of arguments: For design choices (e.g. indemnity vs fixed benefit), always state the trade-off, not just one side.
  • Specific, not vague: "Deferred period of 3–6 months before benefit commences" scores. "Consider a waiting period" does not.
  • Link to insurer perspective: Design choices must be justified from the insurer's risk appetite, not just customer preference.
  • Use product-specific terminology: ADL triggers (LTC), replacement ratio (IP), moratorium underwriting, free cover limits (group) — show you know the product.
  • Distinguish design from pricing: Keep "what the product does" and "how we price it" in separate sections. Many candidates blur these.
