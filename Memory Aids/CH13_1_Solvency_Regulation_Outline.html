USA — State-Based System & Solvency Modernisation
State-Based 50 states + DC + 5 territories · Each state has own regulator NAIC Coordinates standards / model laws · Uniformity of reporting & capital Federal: FIO + FRB FIO monitors & advises (no supervisory role) · FRB oversees systemically important insurers RBC Standard formula · Asset/insurance/business risk factors · Identifies weakly capitalised firms · ≈ MCR under Solvency II PBR (from 2017) Replaces formulaic reserves · Net premium / deterministic / stochastic reserve · Hold highest ORSA Annual self-assessment · Risk management adequacy · ≈ Solvency II ORSA · IAIS ICP 16 compliant SMI (2008) Self-examination of US solvency framework · Capital, governance, group supervision, reinsurance vs EU: RBC ≈ MCR · US reserves formulaic vs SII market-consistent · SII focuses on firm-specific risks

China — C-ROSS Framework
Regulator: CBIRC Supervision · Enforcement · Capital changes C-ROSS: 3 Pillars Pillar I — Quantitative capital (insurance/market/credit risk) Pillar II — Qualitative: rating system + ORSA equivalent + liquidity indicators Pillar III — Transparency & disclosure; systemic risk vs Solvency II Assets at GAAP value (not mark-to-market) Discount yield ≈ GAAP liability yield (not risk-free curve) All insurers use same rules — no internal models allowed Challenges Complex production · Significant extra workload · Pillar II frameworks to build · Streamline reporting
C-ROSS similar to Solvency II but reflects China's younger, developing insurance market.
↓
④
Australia — LAGIC & APRA
Regulator: APRA Risk-based approach · Targets highest-risk areas LAGIC (from Jan 2013) Life & General Insurance Capital · Three pillars · Strong parallels to Solvency II Pillar I: PCA Prescribed Capital Amount · 99.5% VaR over 1 year · Standard method or internal model · Insurance/asset/concentration/operational risk · ≈ SCR Pillar II Good governance & risk management · Individual capital adequacy assessment ≈ ORSA Pillar III Regular disclosure requirements Supervisory adjustment APRA can increase or decrease required capital · ≈ Solvency II capital add-on Equivalence Provisional equivalence with Solvency II (10-year periods)

South Africa — SAM Framework
Regulators PA (Prudential Authority) — licensing, supervision, enforcement FSCA — market conduct, consumer protection, financial literacy SAM (from July 2018) Solvency Assessment & Management · Largely based on Solvency II · Three pillars Pillar I Quantitative · SAM balance sheet & capital · 1-in-200 year loss event · ≈ SCR · Standardised formula or internal model Pillar II Qualitative · Sound governance & risk management Pillar III Regulatory reporting + public disclosures SA adaptations SA risk-free rates · Market risk calibrated to SA · Microinsurance: reduced MCR to widen access Equivalence goal Designed to comply with Solvency II third-country equivalence criteria

Trend Building more robust regulatory & solvency frameworks across the region Min Capital Myanmar Formula-based India · Vietnam · Brunei Risk-based Capital Indonesia Economic Capital + ORSA China · Japan · Malaysia · Australia
Sophistication ranges from simple minimum capital to full economic capital + ORSA. Most regulators moving rightward.

Latin America — Solvency II-Type Adoption
Direction Many countries adopting Solvency II-type frameworks or risk-based capital elements Mexico First in region to adopt Solvency II-like framework Brazil Provisional Solvency II equivalence from 1 Jan 2016 (10 years) · ORSA equivalent introduced Chile Developing risk-based capital approach modelled after Solvency II · ORSA equivalent introduced Consequence Greater focus on risk management expected across the region
