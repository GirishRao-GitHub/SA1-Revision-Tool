Facultative vs Treaty — How Business is Placed
FacultativeEach risk individually negotiated · Cedant & reinsurer each free to accept/reject✓ Flexibility · Covers risks outside treaty · Best terms per risk✗ Time-consuming · No certainty of cover · Delay accepting business
TreatyAutomatic placement within defined scope · Usually ob/ob · Terms pre-agreed✓ Certain cover · Efficient admin · Helps control solvency & growth✗ Inflexible once set up · Terms may restrict insurer's freedom
Fac / FacFac / ObOb / ObCedant perspective first · Reinsurer perspective second · Bulk of business = ob/ob
Treaty must be precise: coverage scope · financial arrangements · obligations of both parties

③
Proportional Reinsurance — Basis (Original Terms vs Risk Premium)
Original Terms (Coinsurance)Reinsurer shares premium & claims in same proportion · Commission from RI to cedantReinsurer sees cedant's premium rates (checks adequacy) · Deposit back possibleRisk PremiumReinsurer sets its own rate independently of cedant's premiumApplied to SI or sum-at-risk · Can be level or yearly-increasing · Guaranteed or reviewable
Sum-at-Risk variantReinsure only excess of benefit over reserve (SAR) · Mainly CI; not usually IP/LTCI
CommissionPaid under proportional (original terms) only · May be profit commission · Rebate = deduction from RI premium
Long-term: quota share or surplus on original terms or risk premium basis · Short-term: quota share on original terms only
↓
④
Quota Share — Same % for Every Risk
MechanismFixed R% of every risk ceded automatically · Simplest to administer · Always treaty
PurposesSpread / reduce parameter risk · Write larger portfolios · Reciprocal businessImprove solvency ratio (net WP ↓) · Satisfy statutory capital req'ts · Finance NBS
LimitationsSame % regardless of risk size (less efficient than surplus for targeting large risks)Cedes a share of profit · Proportion changes apply to NB only unless agreed otherwise
Popular for new products, new territories, new insurers; also for financing (commission = disguised NBS loan)
↓
⑤
Surplus — Variable % per Policy (Long-Term Only)
MechanismFixed monetary retention; cede excess SI above retention · % varies by policy sizePolicies below retention: 0% ceded · Long-term fixed-SI products only (not PMI)
AdvantagesAccepts larger risks · Targets large risks precisely → reduces claims volatility
DisadvantagesLess protection against parameter risk (net share ≠ constant) · Less suitable for financing
Key contrast: Quota Share = uniform %; Surplus = targeted — reinsures exactly the slice above retention
↓
⑥
Non-Proportional (XL) — Caps Claims Above Threshold
Risk XLIndividual claims · PMI portfolios · Layer: £X in excess of £Y
Aggregate XL / Stop LossTotal account for 12 months · Excess point & upper limit as % of earned premiumIn health context aggregate XL = stop loss (morbidity = only peril)Stop loss often unavailable at affordable price
Catastrophe XLSingle event / pandemic · Covers aggregate from one cause · Rarely affordable for pandemic
Indexed limitsStability clause: excess point indexed to inflation · Extra premium payable if not indexed
Proportional within XLRI covers 90% of layer eg → insurer retains moral hazard interest in large-claim control
Non-proportional only short-term & group long-term · No commission (except possible profit commission)
↓
⑦
Financial Reinsurance — Capital & Financing Arrangements
Risk Premium Fin ReCommission = disguised loan · Repaid via higher future RI premiums · Lapse risk on reinsurer
Contingent Loan (Surplus Relief)Loan repaid from VIF profits · If VIF doesn't emerge → reinsurer absorbs loss · No reserving for repayment
UsesNew business strain · Improving free assets · M&A financing
LimitsLittle insurance risk transfer · Ineffective under Solvency II (realistic balance sheet)Financial reinsurance ≠ reduce EV materially · Does reduce EV risk (VIF converted to cash)
Normal method: quota share (original terms). Financial RI off-balance-sheet only under non-realistic regimes

Reinsurance Impact & Badging (SA1)
Tax ArbitrageDifferent tax basis → improve after-tax result for both parties
Capital ArbitrageReinsurer holds less solvency capital per unit risk → cheaper reinsurance rates
New MarketsInsurer: gains experience · Reinsurer: gains profitable share + market credibility
Difference of InterestsInsurer: quantity (write more) · Reinsurer: quality (profitable claims) · Conflict on large settlements
BadgingNon-RI alternative: generalist fronts product; specialist health insurer issues & administersSpecialist takes all risk · Generalist provides brand/distribution · Profit sharing arrangement

Determining Retention Level
Ruin Probability ApproachStochastic model: simulate claims + assets → find retention s.t. P(insolvency) < threshold (eg 0.5%)
Cost Minimisation ApproachRetention ↑ → self-held fluctuation reserve cost ↑, reinsurance cost ↓ · Minimise (a)+(b)
Key DriversUncertainty of future claims · Acceptable P(ruin) · Variance of benefit levels · Size of free assets
LT vs STLT fixed benefit (IP/CI): model claim incidence stochastically · ST indemnity (PMI): model both incidence & amounts
Large insurers with sufficient free assets may dispense with XL for normal policies; only reinsure catastrophes and exceptional SI
