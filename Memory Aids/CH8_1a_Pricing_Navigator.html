Chapter 17 — Pricing (1)
Individual Business — Risk Premiums by Product
IP: Inception/Annuity 5 components: (1) survival probability (eligible to claim) · (2) claim inception rate i_x^d (end of deferred period) · (3) disabled life annuity (EPV of benefit duration) · (4) discount factor · (5) annual benefit amount
IP: Multi-state Tracks cohorts: healthy payers → sick in deferred period → claimants → recovered → dead · Each sub-cohort has own transition probabilities · More sophisticated but data-hungry
CI: Stand-alone Rate = i_x × P(survive survival period) · Sum by illness type (cancer, heart, stroke, other) · Watch double-counting if using population medical records
CI: Accelerated i_x + (1 − k_x)·q_x where k_x = proportion of deaths caused by CI · Double-counting k_x → understates cost → inadequate premiums
LTCI Inception/annuity or multi-state · Multiple benefit levels (home / residential / nursing) · Value incremental benefit at each level · Whole-life disability annuity; no recovery assumed
PMI Risk premium = Σ_k (i_k × AC_k) by age & gender · Adjust for: excesses · NCD structures · unisex weighting (anti-selection risk) · Multi-state if premiums guaranteed >1 year
A&S / Cash Plans A&S: inception/annuity; average rate per lender/scheme · Cash plans: inception rate × average benefit per category; allow for "inertia" (under-claiming) · Approximations common due to data limits
Claim inception rate ≠ sickness inception rate — CI rate = sickness inception × P(remaining sick through deferred period). IP & LTCI share the same two pricing frameworks; CI and PMI have product-specific formulae.

Chapter 18 — Pricing (2)
Group Business — Book Rates, Experience Rating & Credibility
5 Special Features Free cover (no UW to limit) · Broker intermediary control (data quality risk) · Limited insured info → deposit & adjustment premiums · Newcomers/leavers changing risk profile · Flexible benefits (anti-selection on choice)
Book Rate Same methodology as individual but lower: less anti-selection (especially compulsory) + lower expenses (economies of scale) · Risk cells by age, gender, occupation · Adjust historic rates for future trends & inflation
Rating Factors Individual-level: age, gender, smoker, occupation · Group-level: industry, location, size, employer's health attitude · Larger scheme → more weight on experience, less on individual factors
Experience Rating RP = Z·A + (1−Z)·E where Z = credibility (0→1), A = group's own experience rate, E = book rate · Commercially Z often given as 1 for PMI with <100 man-years; IP requires more data · Used for PMI & IP group; individual priced on community rate
Burning Cost Accumulation of recent claims adjusted for exposure changes, benefit changes & medical inflation · Include IBNR & RBNS · For IP: capitalise future claim payments · Earliest feedback on premium adequacy
Community rating (group context) = applying tabular rates without reference to past claims history. Individual business does not use experience rating. Burning cost ≠ risk premium — it is a monitoring/adequacy check tool.

Chapter 19 — Pricing (3)
Other Considerations — From Risk Premium to Office Premium
3 Profit Criteria NPV (best — expressible as % of PV premiums or % of initial commission) · IRR (simpler concept but no unique value if sign changes; can't be related to market share) · Discounted payback period (useful for capital-constrained firms; ignores post-payback cashflows)
Marketability If uncompetitive: revisit product design · distribution channel · profit requirement · or decision to proceed · Re-examine acquisition costs, marginal admin costs & fixed overhead contribution
Competitiveness Monitor competitors' rating factors — finer differentiation → cherry-picking of lighter risks → anti-selection against broader pricer · Fixed expenses create economies of scale → volume matters · Loss leaders: cross-subsidise from other lines; risk is adverse business mix
Reserves & SCR Cost of capital = (IRR − asset yield) × SCR locked in · Reserves in pricing model must match expected regulatory basis (prudent or best estimate as applicable) · Reinsurance can reduce SCR and lower total capital cost
Reinsurance Impact Roles: tech expertise · risk sharing · smoothing profits · NB strain financing · tax/capital arbitrage · If cost > benefit: net cost included in pricing · If arbitrage benefit > cost: price net of reinsurance · Quota share reinsurer may co-approve premium tables
Regulatory Impact Community rating or prescribed basis → pricing constrained · Restriction on rating factors (eg genetic tests, gender ban) → cost reflected in premiums · Premium tax → expense loading · Affects all players equally → no competitive distortion
Premium Reviews 3 review types: (1) new business basis monitoring · (2) reviewable premium adequacy testing · (3) loss-leader reserve adequacy · Assumption change need not force premium change — test significance first
NPV is the preferred primary criterion; use discounted payback period as secondary. Loss leader risk: easy to over-sell cheap product, under-sell the subsidising one. Sensitivity testing runs throughout all stages of pricing.

Chapter 20 — Pricing (4)
Options & Guarantees — Valuation, Assumptions & Resilience
3 Option Types Purchase additional cover without medical evidence · Renew policy at term end (eg CI) · Buy-back: reinstate life cover after accelerated CI payout · Only exercisable when not currently claiming (to limit anti-selection)
Option Cost Cost = proportion exercising × average extra morbidity/mortality over standard · Higher take-up → lower average impairment (healthier lives dilute) · Lower take-up → worse average health of exercisers · Monitoring essential — option can come "into the money" unexpectedly
Valuation Method Cashflow projections: extra benefits + extra premiums (at standard select rates at exercise date) + admin expenses + additional reserves pre & post exercise · Conventional method: 100% take-up → ultimate mortality (conservative, simple) · North American method: realistic take-up rates; harder to obtain data
Extra Assumptions P(option exercised) at each date · Benefit level chosen (if discretionary) · Morbidity of exercisers (eg 150% base or age-rated +5 yrs) · Morbidity of non-exercisers (set so that average = base level) · Additional expenses
Stochastic Modelling Simulate proportions and claim experience in each risk sub-group · Run many scenarios → distribution of option cost → statistical adequacy level · Sensitivity re-run for "what-if" disease prevalence shifts
Guarantees Resilience testing: can premium cover outgoings under adverse scenarios? · Additional reserves in early years when risk lower · Premium loading for cost of guarantee reserves · Disclosure of true cost may drive policyholders to reviewable contracts · Regulatory approval of techniques required
Investment guarantees rarely onerous in H&C (small reserves vs benefit). Exception: pre-funded LTCI. Health options differ from financial options — some policies always "in the money"; restricting exercise dates reduces anti-selection.
