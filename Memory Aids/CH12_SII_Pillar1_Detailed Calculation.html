SCR Inputs — data entry guide. Each sub-module charge is the capital requirement for that specific risk, measured as the loss in net asset value under the prescribed stress scenario (Delegated Regulation 2015/35). Enter values in pounds millions. The balance sheet feeds the market risk charge only automatically — all actuarial sub-module charges must be entered manually from your actuarial workings.
Blue border — auto-populated from Balance Sheet tab (ΔNAV = base − stressed). Recalculates whenever you update the balance sheet.
White border — manual entry. Enter the charge from your actuarial sub-module calculation. Not overwritten by balance sheet changes.
Dashed border — derived / read-only. Computed from inputs above; cannot be edited directly.

Key outputs
SCR = BSCR + SCRop + Adj. The BSCR reflects diversification via the 5×5 Annex IV matrix. SCRop is added without diversification. Adj is the loss-absorbing capacity of TPs and deferred taxes. All SCR charges are derived from the stressed vs base net asset value movement entered on the Balance Sheet tab.
Tier summary — stressed own funds
SCR: ≥50% T1; T3 <15%. MCR: ≥80% T1; no T3. Ancillary own funds require prior supervisory approval and are off-balance-sheet. The reconciliation reserve is the residual of the SII balance sheet equation.
