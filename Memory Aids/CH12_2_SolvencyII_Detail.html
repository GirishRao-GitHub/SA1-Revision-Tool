Solvency II & Capital Requirements
SP1 Ch.22 · SA1 Ch.11–12 DETAIL REFERENCE
① Solvency II — Overview & Scope
Background & Scope
  • Objective: Adequate protection of policyholders; risk-based, harmonised EU-wide approach to capital adequacy, risk management and reporting.
  • Applies to: EU insurance and reinsurance companies with gross premium >€5m or gross TPs >€25m. Operative from 1 January 2016.
  • EIOPA: Provides technical advice to the European Commission. PRA is the UK representative.
  • Transitional arrangements: Up to 16 years for TPs and risk-free rates; formal application to national regulator required in some countries.
  • Global influence: Non-EU countries adopting SII-type requirements. Bermuda & Switzerland = full equivalence. USA, Japan, Australia, Brazil, Canada, Mexico = provisional equivalence.
Business Culture Impact (SA1 Ch.11 §4.2)
  • Not just a reporting framework — a risk management framework affecting capital allocation, risk mitigation and performance management.
  • Influences optimal product mix and design.
  • Affects optimal asset mix — some asset classes more attractive due to lower capital requirements.
  • Corporate structures and M&A activity influenced by availability of diversification benefits.
  • Management information aligned to SII metrics. External disclosures affect market perception.
  • Board and senior management fully engaged — not just compliance teams.
② The Three-Pillar Structure
Solvency II Framework — Three Pillars
Pillar 1 — Quantitative
  • Market-consistent balance sheet
  • Technical Provisions = BEL + Risk Margin
  • SCR — Solvency Capital Requirement
  • MCR — Minimum Capital Requirement
  • Valuation of assets, liabilities, own funds
Covered in detail in SA1 Ch.12
Pillar 2 — Qualitative
  • Governance: Board responsible for SII compliance
  • 4 key functions: Risk Management · Actuarial · Compliance · Internal Audit (clear segregation required)
  • ORSA — Own Risk & Solvency Assessment: identify all risks, risk management processes, quantify ongoing MCR/SCR ability over 3–5yr horizon
  • ORSA must be used by senior management in strategic decisions — "embeddedness" required
  • Capital add-on if ORSA reveals SF inadequacy (exceptional circumstances)
Pillar 3 — Disclosure
  • RSR (Regular Supervisory Report): private to supervisor · annual (summary acceptable under certain conditions) · contains ORSA + QRT
  • QRT (Quantitative Reporting Templates): annual full + quarterly subset (MCR/SCR/assets/TPs/own funds)
  • SFCR (Solvency & Financial Condition Report): public disclosure · annual · published within 3–4 months of year-end
  • Local regulators may impose national specific templates in addition
Pillars 2 & 3 sometimes called "Pillar 5" due to synergies between governance and disclosure.
③ Pillar 1 — Balance Sheet: Assets, BEL & Risk Margin
Solvency II Balance Sheet
Assets
  • 3 components: Investments (equities, bonds, property) · Reinsurance recoveries · Participations (≥20% voting rights)
  • Valuation: Market value (quoted markets) or mark-to-model (unquoted equities, derivatives, property). Fair value = arm's length between knowledgeable willing parties.
  • Prudent person principle: Assets appropriate to nature/term of liabilities · best interests of policyholders · derivatives only for risk reduction / efficient portfolio management · properly diversified · own key risk indicators (not just rating agencies)
  • Reinsurance: Shown as asset (not net of gross liabilities). Adjusted for counterparty default risk (PV of expected losses). Usually small.
Technical Provisions = BEL + Risk Margin
  • BEL (Best Estimate Liability): PV of expected future cashflows (benefits + expenses − premiums) discounted at risk-free yield curve. Best estimate assumptions — no prudential margins. Policy-by-policy or grouped. No floor of zero (BEL can be negative). Contract boundary: up to maturity/expiry for LT; end of policy year for annually renewable ST.
  • Risk Margin: Additional amount to make TP = transfer value. Cost of capital method: project non-hedgeable SCR subset over run-off; multiply by 6% p.a.; discount at risk-free rates. Risk margin must be disclosed separately per line of business (but can allow for diversification benefit).
  • Hedgeable cashflows: TP = market value of replicating portfolio. No separate BEL/RM needed.
  • SLT health: IP, SACI, LTCI → life-like basis. Non-SLT: PMI → non-life basis. Unbundle where feasible. Accelerated CI likely life obligation.
Own funds structure Own Funds = Assets − TPs − Other Liabilities
= Basic Own Funds + Ancillary Own Funds
Tiered: T1 (paid-up share capital) → T3 (subordinated debt)
SCR: ≥50% T1, <15% T3 · MCR: ≥80% T1, no T3
④ Discount Rates — Matching Adjustment & Volatility Adjustment
Base Risk-Free Rate
  • Swap rates where deep & liquid market exists; otherwise government bond rates.
  • Credit risk adjustment applied as downward shift to observed yields.
  • EIOPA publishes monthly for 53 countries (all EEA + key non-EEA).
  • Zero-coupon spot rates with annual maturities; shortest = 1yr, longest = 150yr.
  • Long maturities extrapolated using Smith-Wilson method to a long-term equilibrium rate.
Matching Adjustment (MA)
  • For LT predictable liabilities where matching assets held to maturity (annuity-type, no surrender risk).
  • Discount rate = risk-free rate + MA, where MA = spread on matching assets less fundamental spread (EIOPA publishes).
  • MA = illiquidity premium (credit risk excluded via fundamental spread deduction).
  • Assets ring-fenced, bond-like, held to redemption. Key liability risks: longevity + expenses only.
  • Regulator approval required. Lose right to use for 24 months if eligibility requirements breached (unless restored within 2 months).
Volatility Adjustment (VA)
  • For liabilities not eligible for MA.
  • Purpose: reduce risk of forced asset sales during extreme bond spread movements (pro-cyclical risk).
  • Adds portion of spread (not attributable to credit risk) to risk-free discount rate → reduces liability value in stress.
  • EIOPA publishes VA by currency and by national insurance market (where under particular stress).
  • Subject to liquidity plan and sensitivity analysis. Prior supervisory approval in some EU states.
⑤ Solvency Capital Requirement (SCR)
SCR = VaR of Basic Own Funds at 99.5% confidence over 1 year
Standard Formula
Prescribed stress tests per risk module. SCRi = NAV(best estimate) − NAV(stressed). Subject to floor of 0.
Module SCR aggregation Module SCR = √(ΣΣ Corr_ij × SCR_i × SCR_j)
BSCR = √(ΣΣ Corr_ij × SCR_i × SCR_j) + SCR_int
Overall SCR = BSCR + SCR_op + Adj
Health Insurance Risk Sub-modules
ModuleComponents
SLT HealthDisability/morbidity · Mortality · Longevity · Lapse · Expenses · Revision (IP income annuity changes)
Non-SLT HealthPremium & reserve risk (timing, frequency, severity) · Lapse risk
CATEpidemics · Pandemics · Climate change · Anti-microbial resistance
BSCR correlation matrix (top-level): Market/Default/Life/Health all 25%. Health vs Non-life = 0%.
SCR_op: Based on % of earned premiums and TPs. Added to BSCR with no diversification credit.
Adj: Loss-absorbing capacity of TPs (discretionary benefits) and deferred taxes under stress.
Internal Model
Full or partial alternative to standard formula. Must be approved by supervisory authority. Cannot revert to SF once approved (except with justification).
Approval Tests
  • Use test: Widely used throughout business; significant role in internal governance, risk management, decision-making, ORSA, capital allocation.
  • Statistical quality: Assumptions + data meet minimum quality standards; probability distribution forecasting; expert judgement; materiality; methods of aggregation.
  • Calibration: SCR must be equivalent to VaR at 99.5% over 1 year. Can use different confidence levels/periods if equivalent policyholder protection demonstrated.
  • P&L attribution: Categorisation of risk in model explains causes and sources of actual P&L.
  • Validation: Fully validated by company; regular control cycle review; test against emerging experience.
  • Documentation: Theory, assumptions and limitations clearly documented.
Use test is most challenging. Internal model may be structured freely (e.g. stochastic simulations with copulas rather than stress tests + correlation matrices) provided tests are met.
⑥ MCR & ⑦ Supervisory Intervention Ladder
⑥ Minimum Capital Requirement (MCR)
  • Definition: VaR at 85% confidence over 1 year. Simple linear factor formula (companies don't run VaR — they apply the formula).
  • SLT health / life: Formula based on TPs + capital at risk on death/disability × specified factors (vary by with-profits / unit-linked / conventional without-profits).
  • Non-SLT health / non-life: Formula based on TPs + written premiums × factors by product type.
MCR constraints Floor: MCR ≥ 25% SCR
Cap: MCR ≤ 45% SCR
Absolute min: €3.7m (LT) · €2.5m (ST)
Reported quarterly to regulator
⑦ Supervisory Intervention Ladder
BreachTriggerActions & Timelines
SCRFirst point of interventionNotify immediately · Recovery plan within 2 months · Rectify within 6 months (extendable by 3 months; further extension in exceptional market falls)
MCRUltimate point — authorisation at riskNotify immediately · Short-term finance scheme within 1 month · Rectify within 3 months · Regulator may restrict/prohibit asset disposal · Authorisation withdrawn if unresolved
Breaching SCR: increase own funds or reduce risk profile (reduce SCR). Breaching MCR: last resort — withdrawal of authorisation to write new business.
⑧ Reserves–SCR Interplay · VaR · Active vs Passive Valuation
Reserves–SCR Interplay & VaR (SP1 Ch.22)
ApproachReservesSCR
Prudent reservesLarge margins over best estimateSmall (not risk-specific)
Realistic reservesClose to best estimateLarge risk-based capital requirement
Must be assessed together — adequacy of one cannot be judged without the other.
VaR approach: Stress each risk factor separately → SCR_i per risk. Combine via correlation matrix: Agg. req. = √(ΣΣ Corr_ij × Cap_i × Cap_j). Diversification benefit = sum of SCR_i minus aggregated SCR.
Non-linearity: Capital required is not a linear function of risk drivers (e.g. guaranteed annuity options). Non-separability: Combined events worse than sum of parts (e.g. longevity + expense risk on annuities). Both mean correlation matrix may understate true capital requirement.
Active vs Passive Valuation
Passive
  • Net premium valuation for liabilities (insensitive to yield changes — net premium recalculated).
  • Assumptions "locked in" from policy inception. Non-economic assumptions (e.g. morbidity) updated if experience deteriorates.
  • Assets at book/historic value.
  • SCR as % of best estimate liabilities (simple formula).
  • + Simple, less subjective, stable profit emergence.
  • − Can give false sense of security. Slow to react to market changes. May mask emerging problems.
Active
  • Market-consistent valuation for both assets and liabilities.
  • Assumptions updated frequently; reflect current market conditions.
  • Risk-based SCR (e.g. SII standard formula or internal model).
  • + More informative; better captures impact on financial guarantees and options.
  • − Volatile results. Pro-cyclical risk: falling asset values → higher SCR → forced asset sales → further price falls. Regulators may include amendments (e.g. VA) to dampen this.
Combination of active assets + passive liabilities (or vice versa) can create artificial solvency volatility even when the insurer's true position is unchanged.
⑨ Group Reporting, Equivalence & Chief Actuary Role
Group Reporting & Equivalence
  • Group SCR: Covers group-wide risks including diversification benefits across subsidiaries.
  • Group solvency floor: Sum of MCRs of all individual insurance/reinsurance entities within the group.
  • Each subsidiary: Must independently cover its own SCR and MCR.
  • Group supervisor: Home country top-level company; co-operates with national supervisors. Addresses double use of capital and double leverage.
  • Third country parents: EEA supervisors assess whether equivalent group supervision applies. May require an EEA holding company.
  • Equivalence: Third country regime broadly SII-compliant = equivalent. Full (indefinite) · Temporary (until 31/12/2020 + 1yr extension) · Provisional (10yr periods). Bermuda & Switzerland = full. USA, Japan, Australia, Brazil, Canada, Mexico = provisional.
Chief Actuary Responsibilities under SII (SA1 Ch.11 §4.3)
  • Co-ordinate the calculation of technical provisions.
  • Ensure appropriateness of methodologies and models used, and assumptions made in TP calculations.
  • Assess sufficiency and quality of data used in TP calculations.
  • Compare best estimate against experience.
  • Inform governing body of reliability and adequacy of TP calculations.
  • Express an opinion on the overall underwriting policy.
  • Express an opinion on the adequacy of reinsurance arrangements.
  • Contribute to effective implementation of the risk management system, with particular regard to risk modelling by the firm.
Note: if internal model is used for SCR, responsibility for detailed implementation lies with the risk management function, not the actuarial function.
