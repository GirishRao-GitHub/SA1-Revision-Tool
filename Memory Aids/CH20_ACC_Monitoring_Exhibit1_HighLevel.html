Monitoring Stage of the Actuarial Control Cycle
SP1 Ch.29 · SA1 EXHIBIT I — HIGH LEVEL
The Three Pillars
① Why Monitor?
  • Update assumptions for future experience
  • Monitor adverse trends in experience
  • Compare actual vs expected → corrective action
  • Provide management information for business decisions
  • Inform pricing & reserving adequacy
  • Check underwriting effectiveness & anti-selection
  • Assess reinsurance need / profit share
  • Identify profitable products, markets, channels
② What is Monitored?
  • New business volumes & mix
  • Mortality & morbidity (incidence & termination)
  • Claims amounts
  • Persistency / lapse rates
  • Expenses
  • Investment returns
  • Renewal rates
  • Option take-up rates
  • Customer satisfaction · Reinsurance performance · Solvency
③ How? Data Requirements
  • Reasonable volume — stable & consistent
  • Homogeneous risk groups (balance vs credibility)
  • Same data source, grouping criteria, reliability across groups
  • Period: long enough for volume, short enough for relevance
  • Expenses: ~12 months · Morbidity: 3–5 years
  • Own portfolio + market data + reinsurer data
  • Two strands: A vs E direct · Analysis of Surplus

Analysis of Surplus / Profit & Embedded Value
Long Term Analysis of Surplus Arising
How done: Surplus arising = (A₁ − L₁) − (A₀ − L₀). Breakdown by source: mortality/morbidity, expenses, withdrawals, interest, new business impact.

Why / Uses:
  • Financial effect of A vs E divergences
  • Financial effect of new business
  • Check on valuation data & process
  • Identify non-recurring surplus → distribution decisions
  • Regulatory compliance
  • Trend identification (multi-year)

Analysis of EV Profit: EV
  • Validate calculations, assumptions & data
  • Reconcile successive years
  • Management information
  • Executive remuneration data
  • Publication in accounts (value of new business)
  • Identify profitable lines · Assess return on capital
Short Term Analysis of Profit
How done: Compare actual vs expected claim and expense ratios for cohorts of business.

Why / Uses:
  • Same underlying reasons as surplus for long-term
  • Identify adverse claims trends early
  • Repricing trigger (annually renewable)
  • Assess cohort-level profitability

Key difference from LT surplus:
No long-term reserving basis involved. Focus on current-period ratios rather than movements in actuarial reserves.

EV analysis: Not applicable for pure short-term products (no embedded future profit stream to project).

Customer Value & Iterative Nature
Customer Value
Experience monitoring enables the insurer to provide better value by: identifying over-priced products, improving claims handling efficiency, ensuring underwriting fairness, and aligning product design with actual policyholder needs. Monitoring persistency and complaints data helps gauge perceived value and satisfaction.
Iterative Process
Monitoring is continuous — the ever-changing nature of actual experience means assumptions never fully converge on reality. Historical data becomes less relevant over time. Assumptions must contain margins for model error. Frequency of review depends on class of business (e.g. expenses quarterly; LTC claim rates annually).
