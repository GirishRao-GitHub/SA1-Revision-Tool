IRDAI 2024 Regulation and Master Circular — revision structure map

IRDAI (Actuarial, Finance and Investment Functions of Insurers) Regulations, 2024

F. No. IRDAI/Reg/10/204/2024  |  Notified 20 Mar 2024  |  Effective 1 Apr 2024  |  446 pages  |  Repeals 9 older regulations

Operative chapters — legal framework
Chapter I
Preliminary
Definitions, title, commencement
Short title, applicability to all insurers including reinsurers. Four key objectives. Formal definitions of UPR, PDR, URR, Technical Liabilities, RSM, Solvency Ratio.
Chapter II
Guiding principles
Board policies, schedule references
Principles for actuarial, finance and investment functions. Requires board-approved policies for each function. Routes to Schedules I–V for substantive rules.
Chapter III
Miscellaneous
Powers, repeals
Power to issue circulars, clarifications and directions. Repeals 9 older regulations including ALSM (General Insurance) 2016, ALSM (Life) 2016, Financial Statements Regulations 2002, Appointed Actuary Regulations 2022, Investment Regulations 2016.

Key definitions from Chapter I

UPR — "that part of premium written attributable and to be allocated to succeeding accounting periods"
PDR — "reserve held in excess of UPR, allowing for expectation that UPR will be insufficient to cover claims and expenses in the unexpired risk period"
URR — "aggregate of UPR and PDR" (this is the total unexpired risk liability on the balance sheet)
Technical Liabilities — "UPR + PDR + Claims Reserves (OS + IBNR + IBNER)" for GI/health insurers
Exam point: URR = UPR + PDR. URR is the regulatory solvency concept. UPR is the accounting component. PDR is the actuarial top-up.
Schedules — substantive content (where the real rules are)
Schedule I
Actuarial functions
5 parts
Largest schedule. Life valuation, general/health valuation, appointed actuary, solvency.
Schedule II
Finance functions
3 parts
Financial statements, accounting policies, UPR calculation method, auditor's report.
Schedule III
Investment functions
Investment norms
Approved investments, ALM, exposure norms, valuation of securities, solvency forms.
Schedule IV
Loans and advances
Employee loans
Rules for loans/advances to employees — eligible purposes, aggregate cap (₹1 crore), board policy requirement.
Schedule V
Inspection and returns
Procedural
Procedure for inspection and supply of copies of returns under Section 20(1) of the Insurance Act.

Schedule I — the 5 parts

  • Part I — Definitions: Terms specific to life insurers (math reserves, par/non-par, sum at risk) and general/health insurers (ALAE, IBNR, IBNER, OS Reserves, URR)
  • Part II — Appointed Actuary: Eligibility, appointment process, duties, responsibilities, certification requirements. Every GI/health insurer must have at least 1 additional actuary for pricing/valuation beyond the AA.
  • Part III — Life insurance valuation: Mathematical reserves, surplus, solvency for life insurers
  • Part IV — General/health valuation ★: Assets, Technical Liabilities (URR + Claims Reserve), RSM determination for general insurers and standalone health insurers
  • Part V — Reinsurer solvency: Life reinsurance follows Part III rules; general/health reinsurance follows Part IV rules
Part IV is the primary section for health insurance reserving: URR (UPR + PDR), IBNR, IBNER, Outstanding Claims, and solvency margin for standalone health insurers
Exam point: PDR is maintained at insurer level (minimum zero). Negative PDR in one segment cannot offset another. IRDAI monitors PDR at segmental level for product sustainability.

Schedule II — UPR calculation (Part II, Clause 4)

Marine Hull: 100% of Net Written Premium of preceding 12 months
All other segments (including health): 50% of NWP of preceding 12 months OR proportion of unexpired period to total period of the respective policies
Method must be consistent — change requires prior written approval of Competent Authority
Exam point: The 2024 regulation replaced "1/365th method" (old wording) with "proportion of unexpired period to total period" — conceptually the same, but worded more broadly. The 50% floor is a minimum, not a ceiling.

Master Circular on Actuarial, Finance and Investment Functions of Insurers

Ref: IRDAI/ACTL/CIR/MISC/80/05/2024  |  Dated 17 May 2024  |  270 pages  |  Operational guidance on the 2024 Regulation

Master circular chapters
Chapter 1
Actuarial function
5 sections
Appointed actuary appointment, valuation guidance for life, GI/health and reinsurance. Section III is key for health insurers.
Chapter 2
Finance function
7 sections
Accounting and disclosure requirements, segment-specific guidance, financial statement submission timelines (3 months from year-end or 30 days from board adoption).
Chapter 3
Investment function
8 sections
Investment categories, risk management, concurrent audit, valuation guidelines, disclosure and reporting norms, list of repealed circulars, investment category codes.

Chapter 1 sections

  • Section I — AA / Mentor / Consulting / Certifying Actuary: Appointment procedure, forms (ACTL-AA-1), duties. Every GI/health insurer needs at least 1 additional actuary for pricing/valuation by Dec 2024.
  • Section II — Life insurance valuation: Guidance on mathematical reserves, with-profits fund management (Annexure ACTL-LI-1)
  • Section III — GI and standalone health insurance valuation ★: Operational guidance on URR (= UPR + PDR), IBNR, IBNER, claims reserves. Confirms PDR segmental monitoring.
  • Section IV — Reinsurance valuation: FRB-specific guidance; aggregate-level claims reserve allowed for motor and health if granular data unavailable
  • Section V — Repeal of circulars: Consolidated list of all superseded circulars
Exam point: The Master Circular defines Net Earned Premium = Net Written Premium adjusted for movement in UPR. URR = UPR + PDR. These definitions in the MC mirror Chapter I of the Regulation exactly.

Chapter 2 key points

  • Sec I–II: All insurers must file audited financials within 3 months of year-end or 30 days from board adoption (whichever is earlier)
  • Sec III: Life-specific — bonus philosophy, participating fund disclosures
  • Sec IV: GI/health-specific — accounting for UPR, PDR, IBNR; premium recognition; claims accounting
  • Sec V: FRBs and Lloyd's India specific accounting
  • Sec VI: General provisions for all insurers
  • Sec VII: Procedure for seeking format deviations from IRDAI
Schedule II of the MC lists all repealed non-life / reinsurer / FRB circulars — useful reference to confirm which old guidance is no longer operative
Quick-reference: what replaced what
ALSM (General Insurance) Regulations 2016 → now Schedule I Part IV + Schedule II Part II of the 2024 Regulation
Financial Statements Regulations 2002 (as amended 2021) → now Schedule II of the 2024 Regulation
Appointed Actuary Regulations 2022 → now Schedule I Part II of the 2024 Regulation
Investment Regulations 2016 → now Schedule III of the 2024 Regulation
ALSM (Life Insurance) Regulations 2016 → now Schedule I Part III of the 2024 Regulation
All prior actuarial/finance/investment circulars → consolidated into the Master Circular (17 May 2024)
Exam tip: The 2024 Regulation is principle-based and consolidated. The old regulations had separate documents for solvency, financial statements, investments and appointed actuary. Now all five functions are in one regulation with five schedules, supported by one Master Circular reviewed annually.
