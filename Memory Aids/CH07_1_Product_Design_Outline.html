Provide CashCash = policyholder chooses level & use · Indemnity = insurer negotiates cheaper provider prices · Risk of poor decision with cash
Peace of MindPsychological value of knowing you are protected · Customers often unaware of true risk size · State benefits perceived as more generous than reality
Simplicity & ClarityHealth cover more complex than life insurance · Events variable in severity · Definition problems throughout · Exclusions/conditions add confusion

Cash vs Indemnity & Benefit Form — By Product
IPCash income · Commutable (anti-selection caution) · Proportional/rehabilitation benefit → incentive to return to work
CICash lump sum — cannot be withdrawn · Not linked to actual loss → windfall risk → anti-selection · Can exceed medical cost (angioplasty example)
LTCIUsually cash (not indemnity) — longevity risk + benefit inflation too uncertain · Immediate needs policy: lump sum → income · Pre-funded: cash income at onset of incapacity
PMIIndemnity — insurer pays provider directly · Exceptions: health cash plans · daily hospital benefit · major medical expenses · personal accident (lump sum on specified injury)
IP stops on recovery; CI lump sum does not — key product comparison point

Simplicity & Clarity — Issues by Product
IP Issues(1) Benefit limits (60%) (2) Not always salary-linked (3) Complex UW — occupation/history (4) Exclusions — alcohol/war/travel (5) Objective incapacity — ADWs / FATs / PCAs
CI: SimpleLump sum cannot be withdrawn · Diagnosis/procedure trigger easy to explain · No withdrawal risk
CI: ComplexExclusions · Point-of-claim UW · Non-standard definitions differ by insurer · Tiered benefits = harder to compare + more claims disputes
LTCICash ≠ full indemnity (confusion risk) · Limited periods/amounts · ADL triggers — not doctor's referral — must be explained
PMIUW process misunderstood · Excesses/upper limits · Chronic/cosmetic exclusions · Cost-sharing (coinsurance/excess) · Pre-authorisation · Hospital bands/networks

Guaranteed vs Reviewable — By Product
IPReviewable predominates · Uncertain experience (unemployment trends, economic/social) · Guarantees expensive: reserves required · Selective lapsing limits size of premium increases
CIReviewable more suitable (medical advances rapid) · But competitive pressure has led to guaranteed premium products being sold · Difficult to assess new risk implications
LTCIImmediate needs policy = no guarantee problem (lump sum → income at point of need) · Pre-funded products: reviewable premiums predominate
PMIAnnually renewable → premiums AND policy conditions reviewed each year · Short-term nature eliminates long-term guarantee problem entirely
Customer must be clearly informed of reviewable status and potential for premium change

Insurer: 8 Iterative Design Targets
① Customer AcceptabilityClear benefits/premiums · Meet needs · Marketable · Not overly complex for channel · Sufficient benefits to justify price
② Regulator RequirementsProduct approval before launch · Premium filing (compulsory markets) · Comply with sales/disclosure rules
③ Distributor NeedsConsult sales/marketing early · Appropriate channels · Competitive premiums · Commission/clawback structures · Sales training by actuary
④ Profitability / ROCPremiums cover benefits + expenses + profit · Adequate return on capital · Balance between competitive pricing and margin
⑤ Company CultureConsistency with existing products · Efficiencies: training / admin / systems / printing · Avoid dissatisfying existing policyholders with more favourable new terms
⑥ SystemsRecord inception · Align claims data · Monitor profitability · Group by risk · Add external data · Model/project cashflows
⑦ UnderwritingMedical UW affects design & pricing · Claims UW must be consistent with initial UW criteria and pricing data assumptions
⑧ Risk ManagementAnti-selection/moral hazard control · Reinsurance (cost + risk reduction) · Minimise financing strain · Cross-subsidies · Guarantee costs · Premium review process
Design and pricing are iterative — adjusting each in turn until all targets are simultaneously met

Regulator as Stakeholder & Design Conflicts
Regulator: PrioritiesInsurer solvency · Consumer protection — minimise disadvantages in sales, admin and claims
Consumer ProtectionClear/unambiguous policy wording · Key features document · Value for money vs market · No misrepresentation of title (CI / LTCI)
Sales MonitoringAppropriate product at right level · No undue pressure · Cooling-off periods · Promises match conditions · Sold to intended target market · Premium increases approved (compulsory markets)
Key ConflictsCustomer: guaranteed ↔ Insurer: reviewable · Customer: simplicity ↔ Insurer: needs exclusions/definitions · Customer: full indemnity ↔ Insurer: LTCI uncertainty · Volume ↔ Profitability
All stakeholder needs are interlinked — rarely one set of needs can be discussed without touching others
