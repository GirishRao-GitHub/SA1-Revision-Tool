The Three-Pillar Structure
Pillar 1 — Quantitative Market-consistent balance sheet · Technical Provisions (BEL + RM) · SCR · MCR Pillar 2 — Qualitative Governance · ORSA · Supervisory review · 4 key functions: Risk Mgmt · Actuarial · Compliance · Internal Audit Pillar 3 — Disclosure RSR (private, annual) · QRT (quantitative, annual + quarterly subset) · SFCR (public, annual within 3–4 months of year-end)
Pillars 2 & 3 sometimes called "Pillar 5" due to synergies. ORSA is both a Pillar 2 requirement and a Pillar 3 report.

Pillar 1 — Solvency II Balance Sheet
Assets Market value (quoted) or mark-to-model · Prudent person principle · Reinsurance shown as asset (not net of liabilities) Technical Provisions TP = BEL + Risk Margin · Transfer value to another insurer · Segmented by homogeneous product type BEL PV of best estimate cashflows · risk-free yield curve (EIOPA monthly) · no prudential margins · negative BEL allowed Risk Margin Cost of capital method · 6% p.a. on non-hedgeable SCR · discounted at risk-free rates Own Funds Basic (already exists) + Ancillary (callable) · Tiered: Tier 1 (best) to Tier 3 (worst) · SCR: ≥50% T1, <15% T3 · MCR: ≥80% T1, no T3 SLT vs Non-SLT SLT: IP, SACI, LTCI (life-like) · Non-SLT: PMI (non-life-like)

Discount Rates — Matching & Volatility Adjustments
Base Risk-Free Swap rates (deep/liquid market) or govt bond rates · credit risk adjustment applied downward · EIOPA publishes monthly Matching Adjustment (MA) For LT predictable liabilities held to maturity (annuity-type) · add illiquidity premium to discount rate · reduces liabilities · regulator approval required · ring-fenced assets · no surrender risk Volatility Adjustment (VA) Where MA not eligible · add % of spread to reduce forced-sale risk in crises · EIOPA publishes by currency/market · subject to liquidity plan + sensitivity analysis
MA = insurer-specific (own assets). VA = currency/market-wide. Only one can apply at a time.

Solvency Capital Requirement (SCR)
Definition VaR of basic own funds at 99.5% confidence over 1 year · held in addition to TPs Standard Formula Prescribed stress tests per risk module → SCR_i = NAV(unstressed) − NAV(stressed) · modules combined via correlation matrix → BSCR · add SCR_op + Adj Health Risk Modules SLT health: disability/morbidity · mortality · longevity · lapse · expenses · revision Non-SLT: premium & reserve risk · lapse risk CAT: epidemics · pandemics · climate · antimicrobial resistance Internal Model Full or partial · must pass: use test · statistical quality · calibration · P&L attribution · validation · documentation · cannot revert to SF once approved Capital Add-on Supervisor can impose if SF inadequate for risk profile · exceptional circumstances only

Minimum Capital Requirement (MCR)
Definition VaR at 85% confidence over 1 year · ultimate supervisory intervention point · authorisation withdrawn if breached Formula Simple linear factor formula · SLT: based on TPs + capital at risk on death/disability · Non-SLT: based on TPs + written premiums Floor & Cap MCR ≥ 25% SCR (floor) · MCR ≤ 45% SCR (cap) · Absolute min: €3.7m (LT) / €2.5m (ST) Reporting Calculated and reported quarterly

Supervisory Intervention Ladder
SCR Breach Notify immediately · recovery plan within 2 months · rectify within 6 months (extendable by 3 months or more in exceptional market conditions) MCR Breach Notify immediately · short-term finance scheme within 1 month · rectify within 3 months · regulator can restrict asset disposal · authorisation withdrawn if unresolved
SCR = first point of intervention. MCR = last resort. Ladder of increasingly severe actions.

Reserves–SCR Interplay · VaR · Active vs Passive
Interplay Strong reserves + small SCR (prudent basis) ↔ Weak reserves + large SCR (realistic basis + risk-based capital) · must be considered together VaR Approach 99.5% over 1yr · stress each risk factor separately · combine with correlation matrix (or copulas) · allows for diversification benefit Non-linearity & Non-separability Simple correlation matrix may understate capital · adjust upward for combined event effects Passive Valuation Net premium valuation · locked-in assumptions · book value assets · stable but can give false security Active Valuation Market-consistent · risk-based SCR · more informative but volatile · pro-cyclical risk in crises
SII is an active approach. Passive = older national regimes. Combinations can create artificial volatility.
