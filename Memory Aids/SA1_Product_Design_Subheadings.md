# SA1 Product Design — Exam Reference Sub-headings

> Extracted from IFoA SA1 exam papers: April 2019 – September 2025  
> 80 question-parts tagged with "Product Design" across 14 sittings

---

## 1. Benefit Structure

- Lump sum vs. regular income benefits
- Fixed-term vs. whole-of-life benefit duration
- Indemnity benefits vs. fixed / indexed benefits
- Benefit levels linked to degree of disability / care needs
- Caps, excesses, co-insurance, and monetary limits
- Integration with state provision (NHS, LTC, IP state benefits)
- Index-linking of benefits in payment (RPI, NAE, earnings-linked)
- Death benefits and surrender values as ancillary features
- Replacement ratio design (income protection) — avoiding over-insurance
- Deferred / waiting periods and their rationale
- Benefit cessation triggers (recovery, retirement, death)
- Payment to policyholder vs. directly to care / medical provider
- Rehabilitation benefits and phased return-to-work provisions
- Proportional / partial benefits for partial incapacity

---

## 2. Cover / Claim Definitions

- Clarity and precision of claim trigger definitions
- Activities of Daily Living (ADL) triggers — number and severity of tests
- Single-event vs. ongoing assessment definitions
- Mental impairment triggers
- Critical illness condition definitions — breadth vs. claims pay-out ratio
- Severity-based vs. binary definitions (tiered CI)
- Definitions of "child" for children's options / extensions
- Moratorium-based vs. fully underwritten definition of pre-existing conditions
- Exclusions — standard, special, and occupational
- Policy wording clarity to avoid disputed claims and mis-selling risk

---

## 3. Product Features and Options

- Reviewable vs. guaranteed premiums
- Unit-linked structure with reviewable morbidity charge
- Return of Premium (ROP) option — trigger events and design
- Surrender values (pre-claim and in-claim) — marketability vs. risk
- Guaranteed renewability and continuity provisions
- No-claims discounts
- Waiver of premium during claim
- Policy conversion and upgrade options
- Profit share arrangements (e.g. government / employer contracts)
- Joint / family / group cover options
- Indexation options on sum assured / benefit

---

## 4. Target Market and Customer Needs

- Identifying the target demographic (age, income, occupation, geography)
- Affordability and accessibility of the product
- Microinsurance products — simplified design for low-income markets
- Health inequalities and vulnerable / underserved customer segments
- Financial literacy of target market and product complexity trade-off
- Customer needs vs. insurer risk appetite alignment
- Group vs. individual cover — suitability for employer / government schemes
- Student / niche market products — bespoke design considerations
- Means-tested benefit interaction and gap-filling
- Consumer protection and Treating Customers Fairly (TCF) obligations

---

## 5. Underwriting Approach

- Full medical underwriting vs. simplified / moratorium underwriting
- Point-of-sale underwriting vs. referral to head office
- Anti-selection risk under different underwriting approaches
- Use of AI / algorithmic underwriting tools (e.g. AutoUW)
- Adjusting acceptance criteria to change business mix
- Special terms — loadings, exclusions, postponements
- Group underwriting — free cover limits, individual evidence thresholds
- Minimum participation requirements to control anti-selection
- Impact of underwriting approach on lapse behaviour

---

## 6. Pricing Considerations

- Morbidity assumptions — incidence, severity, duration
- Claim definition impact on pricing
- Lack of credible data — new products, new markets, new conditions
- Community rating vs. risk-rated premiums — advantages and disadvantages
- Rating factors and their relevance (age, gender, occupation, smoker status)
- Reviewable charges — morbidity charge in unit-linked products
- Expense loadings and economies of scale
- Reinsurance costs and availability
- Profit margins and required return on capital
- Premium reviews following adverse experience
- Pricing for options (surrender values, ROP, children's benefits)
- Moral hazard and its impact on expected claims

---

## 7. Distribution and Sales Strategy

- Direct sales vs. broker / IFA intermediary distribution
- Online / digital distribution channels
- Affinity / partnership distribution (e.g. grocery chains, employers, travel companies)
- Commission structures and incentives for intermediaries
- Brand awareness and market positioning for new entrants
- Multi-lingual / multi-currency requirements in international markets
- Costs of customer acquisition vs. retention
- Group scheme distribution through employers
- Regulatory requirements on distribution (e.g. fair presentation, disclosure)

---

## 8. Product Differentiation

- Price competitiveness
- Customer service and claims handling quality
- Value-added services (medical helplines, second opinion, wellness apps)
- Technology and health tracking integration
- Brand strength and reputation
- Distribution exclusivity
- Flexibility of cover / modular product design

---

## 9. Risk Appetite and Fit with Insurer Strategy

- Does the product fit within the insurer's current risk appetite?
- New product / new market entry risks
- Capital strain from new business volumes
- Reinsurance availability as a risk management tool
- Operational capacity — systems, staff, administration
- Impact of product on overall business mix and diversification
- One-off / bespoke products vs. scalable product lines
- Group / government contract risks (pricing, repricing, profit share)

---

## 10. Anti-selection and Lapse Risk

- Anti-selection at point of entry (underwriting design)
- Selective lapsing — healthier lives lapsing, leaving worse pool
- Impact of economic downturns on lapse behaviour
- Surrender values and their effect on anti-selection (pre-claim and in-claim)
- Policyholder communication strategies to reduce lapses
- Policy design features to encourage persistency (e.g. no-claims discount, loyalty benefits)
- Group scheme lapse / renewal dynamics — employer negotiation

---

## 11. Regulation and Consumer Protection

- Regulatory requirements in the target market (licensing, product approval)
- Community rating regulation — mandatory participation and risk equalisation
- Consumer Responsibility / Consumer Duty obligations
- Treating Customers Fairly (TCF) — benefit adequacy, transparency
- Disclosure requirements — policy terms, exclusions, limitations
- Reporting and conduct requirements for new market entrants
- Impact of regulatory change on existing in-force products
- Management information (MI) and monitoring for regulatory compliance

---

## 12. Product Redesign and In-force Management

- Responding to adverse claims experience through product redesign
- Repricing vs. structural redesign of benefits
- Introducing excesses, co-insurance, or benefit caps to restore profitability
- Restricting provider / hospital networks to reduce costs
- Treating existing policyholders fairly during redesign
- Communication strategy for product changes
- Closed book vs. open book management of redesigned products
- Impact of product redesign on reserving and capital requirements

---

## 13. Group Products — Specific Design Considerations

- Employer objectives — staff retention, productivity, legal requirement
- Benefit structure linked to salary / employment grade
- Free Cover Limits and group underwriting thresholds
- Experience rating vs. pooled rating for renewal
- Relationship between employer, insurer, and employee
- Scheme administration — outsourcing vs. in-house
- Multinational / multi-site group scheme complexity
- Auto-enrolment and minimum participation levels

---

## 14. New Market Entry and Product Launch

- Market research — size, growth, competition, regulation
- Lack of local data — use of industry / reinsurer data
- Competitive landscape — existing dominant players
- Cultural, religious, and linguistic considerations
- Partnership / joint venture vs. organic market entry
- Capital requirements and phased build-up of portfolio
- Monitoring of emerging experience — actuarial control cycle
- State provision interaction — gap analysis

---

## 15. Technology and Innovation in Product Design

- Wearables and health tracking in product design
- AI-driven underwriting and its product implications
- Telemedicine and digital health services as value-added benefits
- Data governance and cyber risk in product operations
- Insurtech partnerships — outsourcing pricing and underwriting
- Unit-linked and investment-linked product structures
- Rewards and wellness programme integration
